# Gridware

## Overview

Gridware builds pole-mounted sensor systems (Gridscope) that continuously
monitor electrical grid infrastructure, detecting mechanical, electrical,
and environmental hazards in real time. Headquartered in Walnut Creek, CA,
the company helps utilities prevent wildfires, outages, and equipment
failures through data-driven grid intelligence.

## Technical Focus

- Pole-mounted sensor hardware (Gridscope) for grid monitoring
- Real-time signal processing (electrical harmonics, mechanical stress)
- Active Grid Response (AGR) analytics platform
- Environmental and weather risk correlation
- Edge computing and IoT at utility scale

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised $105M total including a $55M Series B (Nov 2025, led by
Tiger Global and Generation Investment Management) and a $26.4M Series A
(Jan 2025, led by Sequoia Capital). Gridscopes deployed on tens of
thousands of poles; utility partners serve ~40% of U.S. electricity
customers. Founded 2020.

## Hiring

Gridware hires across hardware engineering, embedded systems, data science,
and software. The product requires expertise spanning sensor design, signal
processing, edge computing, and cloud-scale analytics. Experience with
utility-grade hardware, IoT systems, or power systems engineering is
valued.

As a rapidly scaling hardware+software company backed by Sequoia and Tiger
Global, Gridware offers a fast-paced environment with significant field
deployment. The team is still relatively small (under 100), so individual
contributors have high impact across the stack.

## Recent Context

Gridware raised $81M across two rounds in 2025 (Series A in January,
Series B in November), rapidly scaling from pilot deployments to tens of
thousands of installed Gridscope units. Duquesne Light Company won the 2025
Steinmetz Top Innovator Award following its Gridware deployment, and the
company is expanding both U.S. coverage and pursuing global markets.
