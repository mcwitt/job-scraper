# Perennial

## Overview

Perennial (formerly Cloud Agronomics) is the leading
measurement, reporting, and verification (MRV) platform for
soil-based carbon removal. The platform uses advanced digital
soil mapping to quantify soil carbon at scale, drastically
reducing sampling needs while maintaining statistical rigor.
Founded in 2018 by Jack Roswell (CEO), Oleksiy Zhuk, and
David Schurman, Perennial is headquartered in Boulder, CO.

## Technical Focus

- Digital soil mapping and carbon quantification
- Remote sensing and satellite imagery analysis
- Statistical modeling to reduce field sampling 60-70%
- Registry-aligned MRV methodology (Verra VT0014)
- Scalable measurement across any crop or land use
- Carbon credit verification and reporting

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised $25M+ total including an $18M Series A
(May 2022) from GenZero, Microsoft Climate Innovation Fund,
Bloomberg LP, SineWave Ventures, and Augment Ventures.
Approximately 37 employees. Measurement costs below $10/acre
at continental scale.

## Hiring

Perennial hires remote sensing scientists, data engineers,
ML researchers, and soil/agricultural scientists. The team
includes alumni from NASA and Sandia National Labs. Strong
candidates combine geospatial data skills with domain
knowledge in soil science, carbon markets, or agricultural
technology.

## Recent Context

Perennial's digital soil mapping methodology (VT0014)
received Verra approval, enabling projects to reduce
sampling by 60-70%. In Nov 2025, Perennial partnered with
Valor Carbon to accelerate soil carbon projects in Central
Asia. The company continues to expand its global footprint
across regenerative agriculture and carbon credit markets.
