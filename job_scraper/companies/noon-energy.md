# Noon Energy

## Overview

Noon Energy develops carbon-oxygen batteries for long-duration energy storage,
capable of discharging for 100+ hours. Founded in 2018 by ex-NASA engineer
Chris Graves and headquartered in Palo Alto, CA, the technology stores energy
by splitting CO2 into solid carbon and oxygen, then reverses the reaction to
discharge.

## Technical Focus

- Reversible solid-oxide fuel cells
- Carbon-oxygen electrochemistry
- Multi-day energy storage (100-200+ hour discharge)
- Modular containerized energy systems
- Materials derived from CO2 rather than mined critical metals

## Scale & Stage

Private, early-stage. Raised over $50M in venture capital and government
grants, including a $28M round with investors including Aramco Ventures and
Clean Energy Ventures. Currently fundraising Series B. Founded 2018,
technology originated from Mars rover oxygen-extraction research.

## Hiring

As a pre-commercial deep-tech startup, Noon Energy primarily hires
electrochemists, materials scientists, and mechanical/electrical engineers
with relevant fuel cell or battery R&D experience. The team is small and
technically dense.

Given the NASA-heritage technology and academic rigor of the founders,
candidates with research backgrounds in solid-oxide cells, high-temperature
electrochemistry, or energy storage systems are well-positioned. Expect a
high technical bar and hands-on prototyping culture.

## Recent Context

Noon successfully demonstrated its 100+ hour carbon-oxygen battery in a
California Energy Commission-funded pilot, achieving over 200 hours of
discharge cycling. A larger demo targeting 100 kW / 10 MWh is set to come
online soon, with the full commercial system designed for 300 kW / 30 MWh
in a shipping-container form factor.
