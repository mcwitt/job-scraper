# Tenstorrent

## Overview

AI hardware startup led by legendary chip architect Jim Keller, building
RISC-V-based AI accelerators and CPU IP. Pursuing an open-source
alternative to Nvidia's proprietary GPU ecosystem, combining custom
Tensix AI cores with the Ascalon RISC-V CPU.

## Technical Focus

- RISC-V CPU design (Ascalon: 8-wide decode, out-of-order, superscalar)
- Tensix AI accelerator cores with chiplet architecture
- Open-source software stack and tooling
- AI inference and training hardware for data centers
- IP licensing (CPU and AI cores) to partners

## Scale & Stage

Private. Series D at $2B+ valuation ($693M round), followed by
additional $700M+ raise bringing valuation to ~$3.2B. ~1,100 employees
across 4 continents. Licensing deals with Samsung, LG, Hyundai worth
$150M+.

## Hiring

High engineering bar — team includes alumni from Apple (M-series), AMD
(Zen), and Intel. Core roles span silicon design, RISC-V architecture,
compiler/toolchain development, and AI software. Jim Keller's reputation
attracts top-tier hardware talent.

Recent layoffs (7.5% of staff) were described as optimizing team fit
rather than financial cuts. The company is actively hiring for
semiconductor design, software engineering, and infrastructure roles
as it scales toward production.

## Recent Context

Launched Ascalon RISC-V CPU IP targeting data center AI and HPC markets.
Expanding into China through partnership with CoreLab and former Arm
China CEO Allen Wu. Third-generation AI chip using chiplet design
expected to tape out in early 2026.
