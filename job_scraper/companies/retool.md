# Retool

## Overview

Retool builds a low-code platform for creating internal
tools, enabling developers to quickly assemble apps from
pre-built UI components connected to any database or API.
Founded in 2017 by David Hsu (YC W17), headquartered in
San Francisco.

## Technical Focus

- Drag-and-drop app builder with custom JS/SQL support
- AI-powered app generation (AppGen) for enterprise
- Retool Agents — LLM-driven autonomous workflows
- Connectors for databases, REST/GraphQL APIs, and SaaS
- Self-hosted and cloud deployment options
- Role-based access control and audit logging
- Workflow automation engine for backend logic

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C. Raised $45M Series C in July 2022 at
a $3.2B valuation (led by Sequoia Capital); $165M total
funding across 6 rounds. Approximately 450 employees as
of early 2026. Estimated ~$120M ARR as of late 2025, up
from ~$90M at end of 2024. Investors include Sequoia,
Y Combinator, and John and Patrick Collison.

## Hiring

Retool hires primarily in engineering, product, sales,
and go-to-market roles across San Francisco and New York.
Hybrid model with 3 in-office days. The engineering team
(~120 people) builds across frontend, backend, platform,
and developer experience. The bar is high — engineers
participate actively in the interview process, and the
culture emphasizes shipping fast with strong results.

## Recent Context

In 2025, Retool launched AppGen (AI-powered app
generation) and Agents for autonomous workflow execution.
The platform has automated over 100M hours of work for
customers including AWS and Databricks. In December 2025,
Retool signed a multi-year strategic collaboration with
AWS to drive enterprise AppGen adoption at scale. Retool
was named Databricks' 2025 Emerging Partner of the Year.
