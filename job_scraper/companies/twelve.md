# Twelve

## Overview

Twelve is a carbon transformation company that uses
electrochemistry to convert CO2 into fuels, chemicals,
and materials currently made from fossil fuels. Founded
in 2015 at Stanford by Dr. Kendra Kuhl, Dr. Etosha Cave,
and Nicholas Flanders (originally called Opus 12).
Headquartered in Berkeley, CA.

## Technical Focus

- CO2 electrolysis and electrocatalysis
- Proprietary catalyst and reactor systems
- Synthetic gas (syngas), ethylene, and methane production
- Sustainable aviation fuel (E-Jet SAF)
- AirPlant modular production facilities
- Industrial-scale carbon transformation platform

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C. Raised $645M in 2024 led by TPG Rise
Climate ($400M project equity, $200M Series C, $45M credit
facilities). Additional $83M in Series C and project
funding in Feb 2025 from Amazon Climate Pledge Fund,
Mitsui, and Development Bank of Japan. Total funding
~$728M. Approximately 270 employees as of early 2026.

## Hiring

Twelve hires scientists, engineers, and operations staff
across electrochemistry, chemical engineering, process
engineering, manufacturing, and software. The team values
interdisciplinary collaboration and mission-driven
candidates comfortable with hardware scale-up. Culture
emphasizes climate urgency and diversity of perspectives.

## Recent Context

AirPlant One facility under construction in Moses Lake, WA,
targeting 50,000 gallons/year of SAF. Secured a 14-year SAF
purchase agreement with a major European airline group for
260 million gallons. United Airlines invested through its
Sustainable Flight Fund (May 2025). Partnerships with
Alaska Airlines and the U.S. Air Force for CO2-derived
fuels. Backed by investors including TPG, Amazon, DCVC,
and the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative.
