# Cerebras Systems

## Overview

AI hardware company building wafer-scale chips for deep learning
training and inference. The Wafer-Scale Engine (WSE) is the
largest chip ever built, designed to eliminate memory and
communication bottlenecks in AI workloads. Based in Sunnyvale, CA.

## Technical Focus

- Wafer-scale chip design (WSE-2, WSE-3)
- CS-2 and CS-3 AI compute systems
- Compiler and runtime for wafer-scale architecture
- Sparse computation and large model support
- Inference-optimized hardware and software stack

## Scale & Stage

Private. ~500 employees. Multiple funding rounds, IPO filing
in progress. Revenue from system sales to national labs,
pharma, and AI companies. Competing with NVIDIA on specialized
AI workloads.

## Hiring

Hiring across hardware engineering, compiler/systems software,
and ML. Hardware roles require chip design or verification
experience. Software roles emphasize compilers, distributed
systems, or ML frameworks. Unique engineering challenges due to
novel architecture — not a typical chip company.

## Recent Context

Pushing inference as a key differentiator — claiming fastest
inference speeds for large language models. Building out cloud
inference service. Navigating competitive pressure from NVIDIA
and custom silicon from hyperscalers.
