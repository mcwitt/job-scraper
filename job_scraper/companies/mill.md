# Mill

## Overview

Mill makes a kitchen food recycler that dries, shrinks, and
de-stinks food scraps overnight, turning them into chicken feed
and garden nutrients. Founded in 2020 by Matt Rogers and Harry
Tannenbaum, both formerly of Nest (where they built the Nest
Learning Thermostat). Headquartered in San Bruno, California.
Named one of TIME's Best Inventions of 2023.

## Technical Focus

- Consumer hardware: countertop food recycler with drying,
  grinding, and odor-elimination systems
- Embedded software and firmware for appliance control
- Cloud platform, fleet management, and AI-driven analytics
- Computer vision and AI for food waste classification
- Mobile and web application development
- Commercial-scale food recycling hardware

## Scale & Stage

Private. Raised approximately $100M+ across multiple rounds,
including a Series C led by Prelude with participation from
Breakthrough Energy Ventures, GV, and Amazon. Reached $20M
in annual revenue by early 2025. Approximately 200-500
employees. Founded 2020.

## Hiring

Mill hires across hardware engineering (mechanical, electrical,
embedded), software (cloud, mobile, test automation), product
design, and operations. Roles span the full product lifecycle
from concept through manufacturing and post-sale analytics.
The culture is described as fast-paced and self-directed, with
a small-company feel where individual contributors have broad
impact. Strong Nest DNA in the founding team sets a high bar
for consumer hardware and software craft.

## Recent Context

In 2025, Mill launched Mill for Workplace, expanding beyond
homes into offices, hotels, universities, and municipal
buildings. Early enterprise customers include Vornado Realty
Trust and Duolingo. Mill partnered with Whole Foods to turn
commercial food waste into chicken feed. The City of Phoenix
deployed 25+ Mill units across government buildings in the
nation's largest municipal deployment. Mill also became
available through Amazon for direct consumer purchase.
