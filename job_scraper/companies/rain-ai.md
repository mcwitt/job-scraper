# Rain AI

## Overview

Semiconductor startup developing neuromorphic processors inspired
by the human brain for ultra-low-power AI inference at the edge.
Uses analog in-memory computing to eliminate data movement,
targeting IoT, wearables, and robotics. Founded in 2017, based in
San Francisco. YC alum (W18).

## Technical Focus

- Analog neuromorphic processing unit (NPU) design
- In-memory compute architecture (<1 uW/operation)
- Equilibrium Propagation algorithm for analog AI training
- Edge AI inference (100x efficiency vs GPUs claimed)
- Target applications: computer vision, audio, sensor fusion

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series A. Raised ~$146M total across seed and multiple
Series A tranches. Last round was $8.1M Series A-IV in February
2025. ~49 employees. Planned $150M Series B (led by Sam Altman)
collapsed in Q1 2025 due to technical delays and missed
milestones. Reportedly exploring asset sale as of mid-2025.

## Hiring

Company is in a precarious state following the collapsed Series B
and reported asset sale process. Was hiring chip designers, analog
engineers, and ML researchers. Any current hiring is uncertain
given financial situation.

Notable backer history: Sam Altman invested personally and OpenAI
had committed to purchasing $51M in Rain chips, though this deal
preceded the funding collapse.

## Recent Context

Shipped first-generation NPU evaluation units to select partners
in October 2024 (not commercial scale). Planned $150M Series B
collapsed in Q1 2025. Reports in May 2025 indicated the company
was up for sale, with potential acquirers including OpenAI, Nvidia,
and Microsoft. Current operational status is unclear. The company
illustrates the difficulty of analog chip startups competing with
the capital intensity of semiconductor development.
