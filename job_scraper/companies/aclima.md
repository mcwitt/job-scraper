# Aclima

## Overview

Aclima builds mobile sensor networks and a data platform that maps air
pollution at the block-by-block level. Headquartered in San Francisco, the
company deploys sensor-equipped vehicles to collect hyperlocal environmental
data, translating billions of measurements into actionable intelligence for
governments, businesses, and communities.

## Technical Focus

- Mobile air quality sensor hardware and calibration
- Large-scale environmental data collection and processing
- Geospatial analytics and pollution mapping
- Cloud data platform and visualization tools
- Partnerships with Google Cloud for data infrastructure

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised approximately $65-88M total (sources vary). Small team
(51-200 employees). Primary revenue from government contracts and enterprise
partnerships. Founded 2010.

## Hiring

Aclima hires across sensor engineering, data science, geospatial analytics,
and software platform development. The intersection of environmental science
and data engineering is central to most technical roles. Experience with
IoT sensor networks, scientific instrumentation, or large-scale geospatial
data pipelines is valued.

As a mission-driven company focused on environmental justice, Aclima
attracts candidates passionate about climate and public health. The team
is relatively small, so individual contributors have significant impact
and breadth of responsibility.

## Recent Context

In mid-2025, Aclima vehicles began collecting data for California's
landmark $27M Statewide Mobile Monitoring Initiative (SMMI), mapping air
quality in 64 pollution-burdened communities reaching 5.2 million residents.
The project runs through June 2026, when collected data will become publicly
available.
