# insitro

## Overview

Machine learning-driven drug discovery company using AI to model
disease biology and discover new therapeutics. Founded in 2018 by
Daphne Koller (Stanford CS professor, Coursera co-founder).
Headquartered in South San Francisco.

## Technical Focus

- ML models for predicting molecular properties and drug behavior
  in vivo (ChemML platform)
- High-content imaging with pooled CRISPR screening (POSH/CellPaint-
  POSH platform, validated in Nature Communications Dec 2025)
- Large-scale iPSC disease modeling capturing patient genetic and
  phenotypic diversity
- Data flywheel integrating wet-lab data generation, ML training,
  and experimental validation in a continuous loop

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C. Raised $643M+ total from a16z, GV, Arch Venture
Partners, and others. ~230-270 employees after 22% workforce
reduction in May 2025 to extend runway into 2027.

## Hiring

Roles span ML/AI research, computational biology, data engineering,
and wet-lab science. Engineering positions emphasize experience with
large-scale data pipelines, deep learning for molecular/biological
data, and high-performance computing. Strong publication record
valued for research roles.

## Recent Context

Partnered with Eli Lilly (Sep 2025) on ML models for small molecule
pharmacology prediction. Extended Bristol Myers Squibb collaboration
leveraging ChemML platform. Acquired CombinAbleAI in early 2026 to
complete AI drug design capabilities. Pipeline advancing toward
first-in-human readouts in metabolism, oncology, and neuroscience.
