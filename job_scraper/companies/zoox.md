# Zoox

## Overview

Autonomous vehicle company building purpose-built robotaxis, acquired
by Amazon for $1.3B in 2020. Unlike competitors retrofitting existing
cars, Zoox designs vehicles from scratch for driverless operation:
bidirectional, four-passenger pods with carriage seating, no steering
wheel, and 360-degree sensing. Headquartered in Foster City, CA.

## Technical Focus

- Purpose-built autonomous vehicle design and manufacturing
- Full-stack autonomy: perception, prediction, planning, controls
- 360-degree sensor fusion (LiDAR, cameras, radar)
- Fleet management and ride-hailing operations at scale
- Vehicle hardware: active suspension, four-zone climate, per-seat UI

## Scale & Stage

Amazon subsidiary. ~2,600-4,200 employees (sources vary). Fully funded
by Amazon; no external fundraising since acquisition. Operating
robotaxi services in Las Vegas and San Francisco as of late 2025, with
expansion to Dallas, Phoenix, and other cities planned for 2026.

## Hiring

Large engineering org spanning AV software, vehicle hardware, and
operations. Hiring across perception, planning, ML, simulation,
embedded systems, and manufacturing. Amazon backing provides stability
and compute resources (AWS).

Expect a mix of automotive and tech culture. The team has grown
significantly post-acquisition, with Amazon investing heavily in
scaling from prototype to production fleet.

## Recent Context

Launched public robotaxi service in Las Vegas (September 2025) and
San Francisco (November 2025). Completing 450,000 paid rides per week
as of early 2026. Plans to start charging in Las Vegas in early 2026.
Announced Uber partnership in March 2026 to make Zoox available via
the Uber app in Las Vegas, with LA planned next. Expanding testing to
Phoenix and Dallas. Debuted production vehicle at CES 2026 with full
360-degree sensing suite.
