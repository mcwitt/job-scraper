# Oklo

## Overview

Advanced fission company developing fast neutron
microreactors that run on recycled nuclear fuel. Founded
in 2013 by Jacob DeWitte (CEO) and Caroline Cochran (COO).
Headquartered in Santa Clara, California. Public company
(NYSE: OKLO).

## Technical Focus

- Fast neutron reactor design (Aurora powerhouse, 15-75 MWe)
- Nuclear fuel recycling from spent fuel waste
- Compact modular reactor manufacturing
- Advanced nuclear licensing and regulatory
- Heat-to-electricity conversion systems

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: OKLO). ~$1.2B cash on hand entering 2026.
~150-250 employees and growing. Pre-revenue; targeting
first operational plant by late 2027 or early 2028.
Stock up ~264% over trailing twelve months.

## Hiring

Growing across nuclear engineering, software, regulatory,
and operations. Y Combinator alumni culture with startup
pace despite public company status. High technical bar
for reactor design and licensing roles.

## Recent Context

Signed deal with Meta to support 1.2 GW nuclear buildout
in Ohio. Federal backing secured for $1.7B fuel recycling
project in Tennessee. Criticality test at Idaho National
Laboratory targeted for July 2026. Positioned as key
player in the AI data center power demand wave.
