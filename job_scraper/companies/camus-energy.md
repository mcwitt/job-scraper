# Camus Energy

## Overview

Camus Energy builds grid orchestration software that gives utilities
system-wide visibility and control over distributed energy resources.
Founded by former Google engineering leader Astrid Atkinson, the
company focuses on helping smaller and mid-sized utilities manage the
transition to a zero-carbon grid.

## Technical Focus

- Grid orchestration and distributed energy resource management
- Real-time monitoring, analytics, and forecasting for utility grids
- DER integration and dispatch optimization
- Large-scale distributed systems architecture applied to grid operations

## Scale & Stage

Private, early-stage. Raised ~$29M total (Series A extended to $26M+,
co-led by Congruent Ventures and Wave Capital). Small team (estimated
30-60 employees). Headquartered in San Francisco. Founded 2019.

## Hiring

Camus Energy hires engineers with strong distributed systems
backgrounds, often from tech companies, who want to apply their skills
to climate and energy. CEO Astrid Atkinson spent 15 years at Google
leading framework infrastructure engineering, and the team reflects
that pedigree in systems design rigor.

The company values integrity, inclusivity, and collaborative
ownership. As a small team tackling a complex infrastructure problem,
engineers have broad scope and direct customer impact. Roles span
backend systems, data engineering, and grid-facing software.

## Recent Context

Camus Energy has been mentioned in the context of data center grid
integration and flexible load management, an increasingly important
area as data center power demand surges. The company continues to
focus on bringing modern software infrastructure practices to utility
grid operations.
