# KoBold Metals

## Overview

KoBold Metals uses AI and machine learning to discover
critical minerals (copper, lithium, nickel, cobalt) needed
for the energy transition. Founded in 2018 by Kurt House,
Josh Goldman, and Jeff Jurinak. Headquartered in Berkeley,
California. The company's Machine Prospector platform
integrates geological, geochemical, and geophysical data
to identify mineral deposits that traditional methods miss.

## Technical Focus

- Machine Prospector AI platform for mineral exploration
- Statistical modeling and big data aggregation
- Geoscience data processing and integration
- Machine learning for subsurface pattern recognition
- Exploration program design and uncertainty modeling
- Data pipelines across geological datasets

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series D. Raised over $1.2B total, including a
$537M Series D in January 2025 at a $2.96B valuation.
Backed by Breakthrough Energy, Andreessen Horowitz,
T. Rowe Price, and BHP. Approximately 280 employees
as of early 2026. Over 60 active exploration projects
across four continents (Africa, North America, Australia,
Asia). Invests $100M+ annually in exploration.

## Hiring

KoBold hires data scientists, software engineers, and
geoscientists who co-lead exploration programs together.
Engineers come from top tech companies; geologists bring
deep domain expertise. The culture blends Silicon Valley
ML engineering with field geology. Roles span ML/AI,
data engineering, geoscience, and mining development.
High bar for quantitative rigor and comfort with
scientific uncertainty.

## Recent Context

KoBold's flagship Mingomba copper deposit in Zambia is
advancing toward a $2B underground mine projected to
produce 300,000 tonnes of copper annually, with shaft
sinking planned for early 2026. The company anchored the
Zambia-Lobito rail corridor project with Africa Finance
Corporation. In Ontario, KoBold's partnership with Libra
discovered 20+ lithium-bearing pegmatite outcrops. KoBold
also signed exploration agreements with the DRC in 2025.
