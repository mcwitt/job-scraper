# Base Power Company

## Overview

Base Power deploys distributed residential battery
systems that provide backup power to homeowners while
aggregating into a virtual power plant for grid
services. Founded in 2023 by Zach Dell (CEO) and
Justin Lopas (COO), the company is headquartered in
Austin, TX. Homeowners pay $695-$995 upfront for a
leased 25 or 50 kWh battery installed at their home.

## Technical Focus

- Residential battery deployment and management
- Virtual power plant aggregation and grid dispatch
- Battery manufacturing (factory near Austin, TX)
- ERCOT market participation and grid services
- Software for fleet management and optimization

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C ($1B in Oct 2025) led by Addition
with a16z, Lightspeed, Thrive, and Contrary. Total
raised ~$1.28B at a $4B post-money valuation. Over
100 MWh deployed in under two years, deploying at
20 MW/month. Revenue projected to reach $70M in 2026.
Founded 2023.

## Hiring

Base Power hires across hardware engineering, software,
manufacturing, field operations, and energy markets.
The company is scaling rapidly from startup to
industrial operations, with a battery factory ramping
near Austin and expansion planned beyond Texas.

## Recent Context

In October 2025, Base Power closed a $1B Series C to
build a second U.S. battery factory and expand beyond
Texas. In December 2025, Base signed a deal with Texas
Farmers Cooperative for networked residential batteries
to provide outage protection on the ERCOT grid.
