# Anyscale

## Overview

Company behind Ray, the open-source distributed computing framework widely
used for ML training, serving, and data processing at scale. Anyscale offers
a managed Ray platform that simplifies running distributed AI workloads
across cloud providers. Founded by the Ray creators at UC Berkeley.

## Technical Focus

- Ray framework: distributed execution for training, serving, and data
- Anyscale Runtime: optimized Ray engine for faster workloads
- Managed compute clusters with MLflow/W&B integration
- Multi-cloud support (AWS, GCP, Azure)

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C. Raised $281M total ($199M Series C in 2021 at ~$1B
valuation). ~350-570 employees (estimates vary). Secondary market valuation
around $600M as of late 2025. Ray transferred to PyTorch Foundation in 2025.

## Hiring

Strong distributed systems and ML infrastructure culture. Values experience
with large-scale distributed computing, cluster management, and ML
pipelines. Open-source contributor background is a plus given Ray's
community-driven development model.

Platform engineering, cloud infrastructure, and developer relations roles
available alongside core systems work. The Azure partnership (GA expected
2026) is driving growth in cloud-native infrastructure roles.

## Recent Context

Ray joined the PyTorch Foundation in late 2025, becoming a neutral
industry-standard framework for AI workloads. Anyscale launched on Azure in
private preview (November 2025) in collaboration with Microsoft, with GA
expected in 2026. New Anyscale Runtime offers drop-in Ray compatibility with
improved performance.
