# Group14 Technologies

## Overview

Group14 Technologies is the leading commercial manufacturer of silicon-carbon
composite anode materials for lithium-ion batteries. Founded in 2015 and
headquartered in Woodinville, WA, the company's SCC55 technology enables
batteries to charge faster and store up to 50% more energy than traditional
graphite anodes.

## Technical Focus

- Silicon-carbon composite anode materials (SCC55)
- Battery materials science and electrochemistry
- High-volume manufacturing processes for active materials
- Fast-charging optimization (sub-10-minute charging)
- Cycle life engineering (1,500+ charge cycles)

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised over $1 billion total including a $463M round in 2025 led
by SK with participation from ATL, Microsoft, Porsche, and OMERS. Operates
three factories: two in the U.S. (Woodinville and Moses Lake, WA) and one
in South Korea (joint venture with SK). Received $100M and $200M DOE grants.
Founded 2015.

## Hiring

Group14 hires across materials science, chemical engineering, manufacturing,
and software. Given the capital-intensive scale-up phase, they need people
who can bridge R&D and production. Experience with battery materials,
electrochemistry, or high-volume manufacturing is valued.

The company culture reflects its Pacific Northwest roots with a
mission-driven focus on decarbonizing transportation. As a growth-stage
hardware company, roles span lab work, factory operations, supply chain,
and data/software to support manufacturing analytics.

## Recent Context

In 2025, Group14 raised $463M and announced a collaboration with BASF on a
market-ready silicon anode solution with 4x higher capacity than graphite.
The company's SCC55 material is now powering millions of devices worldwide,
with 150+ customers testing product from the South Korea factory.
