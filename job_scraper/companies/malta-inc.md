# Malta Inc

## Overview

Malta develops utility-scale, long-duration energy storage using
pumped-heat technology. Spun out of Alphabet's X moonshot factory, the
system uses a steam-based heat pump to store energy as heat in molten salt
(565C) and cold in water, with discharge durations from 8 hours to
multiple days.

## Technical Focus

- Pumped-heat thermal energy storage systems
- Molten salt heat storage and cold-side thermal management
- Multi-stage heat pump engineering (COP > 1.6)
- Long-duration energy storage (8 hours to 8+ days)
- Grid-scale power dispatch and integration

## Scale & Stage

Private. Raised ~$167.5M total through Series B. Backed by Breakthrough
Energy Ventures, Siemens Energy Ventures, Alfa Laval, Proman, Chevron
Technology Ventures, and Piva Capital. ~17 employees, headquartered in
Cambridge, MA. Pre-commercial stage with first pilot under development.

## Hiring

Malta is a small, deep-tech team focused on bringing a novel energy
storage technology from pilot to commercial scale. Roles tend toward
mechanical engineering, thermodynamics, heat exchanger design, and power
systems integration. The team is heavily technical given the company's
stage.

Candidates should expect a research-to-deployment environment where
engineering rigor matters. Experience with thermal systems, industrial
heat exchange, or power plant engineering is highly relevant. The small
team size means significant individual impact and ownership.

## Recent Context

In mid-2025, Malta launched a commercial-scale demonstration plant in
Puertollano, Spain, with construction underway and grid connection
targeted by end of 2026. The company continues to develop its technology
with partners including Spanish utility Cox, supported by financing from
Siemens Energy Ventures and Alfa Laval.
