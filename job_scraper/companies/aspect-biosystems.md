# Aspect Biosystems

## Overview

Aspect Biosystems is a bioprinting company that develops
3D-bioprinted tissue models for drug discovery and
regenerative medicine. Founded in 2013 by Konrad Walus
and Samuel Wadsworth at UBC, headquartered in Vancouver,
BC, Canada. Aspect's microfluidic bioprinting platform
creates functional human tissue constructs used as
preclinical models to improve the predictivity of drug
testing and reduce dependence on animal models.

## Technical Focus

- Microfluidic 3D bioprinting hardware and software
- Engineered human tissue models (lung, kidney, liver,
  cardiac)
- Preclinical drug testing and toxicology platforms
- Analytical development for tissue characterization
- Computational biology and bioinformatics for tissue
  readouts

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C stage. Raised over $100M CAD including
a large round backed by Johnson & Johnson Innovation,
JJDC, and others alongside Dimension Capital. ~100–150
employees. Headquartered in Vancouver with partnerships
with major pharma companies.

## Hiring

Aspect hires scientists in analytical development,
computational biology, and bioprinting-adjacent research.
General applications are common given limited open
headcount at any given time. Roles suit candidates
interested in the intersection of hardware, biology, and
drug development services.

## Recent Context

Aspect Biosystems continues to forge partnerships with
major pharmaceutical companies for preclinical tissue
model programs and is expanding its tissue platform to
new organ types. The company is also developing
therapeutic candidates using its bioprinted constructs
for regenerative medicine applications.
