# AMP Robotics

## Overview

AI and robotics company modernizing recycling
infrastructure by automating waste sorting and
material recovery. Founded in 2015 by Matanya
Horowitz. Headquartered in Louisville, Colorado.
Rebranded to "AMP" in 2024, dropping "Robotics"
to reflect its expanded portfolio. Tim Stuart
(former COO of Republic Services) joined as CEO
in 2024.

## Technical Focus

- AMP Neuron: computer vision and deep learning
  platform trained on 50B+ real-world object
  images for recyclable material identification
- AMP Cortex: high-speed robotic sorting systems
  picking 80+ items per minute
- AMP Vision: modular computer vision for
  monitoring material flow across facility stages
- AMP ONE: fully integrated AI-powered sortation
  facility solution
- Edge AI inference on industrial conveyor lines
- ML models for plastics, cardboard, paper, cans,
  cartons, and other packaging types

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series D ($267M total raised). Investors
include Congruent Ventures (lead), Sequoia Capital,
XN, Wellington Management, Blue Earth Capital,
Liberty Mutual Investments, and CalSTRS. ~150-200
employees. 400+ AI systems deployed across 100+
clients in North America, Asia, and Europe.

## Hiring

Engineering roles include robotics software,
robotics research, DevOps, QA, and full-stack web.
Strong robotics and applied ML focus. Hardware and
software integration experience valued. Based in
Louisville, CO with field deployment work.

## Recent Context

Raised $91M Series D in 2025. Partnered with Waste
Connections to operate a greenfield recycling
facility in Commerce City, CO, commissioned in
early 2026, processing 62K tons of single-stream
recycling annually. Acquired RDS of Virginia in
September 2025. Featured in Fortune for making
recycling economically viable through AI.
