# Stem Inc

## Overview

Stem provides AI-driven software and services for planning, deploying,
and operating clean energy assets. The company's PowerTrack energy
management platform optimizes battery storage and other distributed energy
resources, helping asset owners maximize revenue through intelligent
dispatch and market participation.

## Technical Focus

- PowerTrack energy management system (EMS)
- AI/ML-driven battery storage optimization
- Energy market bidding and dispatch automation
- Clean energy asset monitoring and analytics
- Edge hardware for storage system control

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: STEM). FY2025 revenue of $156.3M (up 8% YoY), with
software/services/edge hardware revenue growing 25% to $141.4M. First
full-year positive adjusted EBITDA ($6.7M). ~$48.9M cash on hand. FY2026
guidance targets $10M-$15M adjusted EBITDA and $65M-$70M year-end ARR.

## Hiring

Stem hires software engineers, data scientists, and energy market
specialists. Core engineering work centers on the PowerTrack platform,
including real-time optimization algorithms, market integration, edge
computing, and cloud infrastructure. Experience with energy storage
systems, electricity markets, or ML for time-series optimization is
valued.

The company's shift toward a software-first model under CEO Arun
Narayanan (appointed January 2025) emphasizes product engineering and
platform scalability. Candidates with SaaS platform experience who can
apply it to energy domain problems are well-positioned.

## Recent Context

Under new CEO Arun Narayanan, Stem achieved its first full-year positive
adjusted EBITDA in 2025 and accelerated software revenue growth. The
company won contracts for German utility-scale battery projects totaling
100 MWh (operations expected summer 2026) and partnered with a clean
energy asset owner to optimize a Southern California storage portfolio.
