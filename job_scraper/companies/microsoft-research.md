# Microsoft Research

## Overview

One of the largest industrial research organizations in the world,
with over 1,000 researchers and engineers across labs in Redmond, New
York, New England (Cambridge, MA), Cambridge (UK), Asia (Beijing,
Shanghai), and others. Operates as an open academic-style research
environment within Microsoft, with $10-14B annual R&D spend across
the company. Publishes extensively and collaborates with universities.

## Technical Focus

- Foundation model research (LLM theory, reasoning, planning)
- Agentic AI and autonomous systems (context engineering, tool use)
- AI for science (drug discovery, climate, materials)
- Responsible AI (hallucination detection, privacy, fact-checking)
- Reinforcement learning for post-training and reasoning
- Low-power optical interconnects and computing infrastructure
- Healthcare AI (Microsoft Health Futures)
- Economics and computation

## Scale & Stage

Division of Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT). 1,000+ researchers and
engineers. Labs worldwide. Not a startup -- offers industrial
research lab stability with academic freedom and publication culture.

## Hiring

Hires researchers (PhD expected), research engineers, and interns.
Internship program pairs doctoral students with mentors for 12-week
projects. Roles span AI/ML, systems, programming languages, HCI,
economics, healthcare, and science. Strong publication record
typically expected for researcher roles. Research SWE roles available
for those without PhDs, particularly in the AI Frontiers lab
(generative AI, agents).

## Recent Context

2025 research focused on agentic AI, LLM theoretical foundations,
and AI for science. AI Frontiers lab hiring research SWEs for
generative AI in Redmond and NYC. 2026 Research Fellowship program
focuses on agentic AI and human-agent collaboration. Published global
AI adoption report showing widening digital divide. Continued
investment in responsible AI tooling and privacy research.
