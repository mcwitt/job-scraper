# Kevala

## Overview

Energy data analytics company building a cloud-based platform for
electrical grid planning and infrastructure investment. Uses AI and
comprehensive utility data to help grid operators plan for
electrification, renewable energy adoption, and extreme weather
events. Founded in 2014, based in San Francisco.

## Technical Focus

- Grid analytics platform powered by public and proprietary utility
  data
- AI/ML models for load forecasting and grid planning
- Distributed energy resource (DER) siting and valuation
- Carbon intensity analysis and electrification impact modeling
- Integrated grid planning tools for utilities and regulators
- Data infrastructure (Hadoop, PostgreSQL)

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series A. Raised $21M Series A in 2021 ($36M total). ~53
employees across North America, Europe, and Asia. ~$15M annual
revenue. Funded by C5 Capital and Thin Line Capital.

## Hiring

Small team working at the intersection of energy systems and data
science. Likely hiring data engineers, ML engineers, and energy domain
specialists. The platform requires both deep grid expertise and
software engineering skills. Remote-friendly with employees across
multiple continents.

## Recent Context

Building out advisory and professional services as grid resiliency
projects accelerate globally. Works with US Department of Energy on
solar deployment analytics. Focus on electrification planning as EV
adoption and building electrification drive grid investment needs.
