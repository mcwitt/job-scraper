# Cruise

## Overview

Former robotaxi company, now integrated into General Motors as an
autonomous driving division focused on personal vehicles. Originally
founded in 2013 and acquired by GM in 2016. GM shut down the Cruise
robotaxi program in December 2024 after spending ~$10B, pivoting to
apply Cruise's autonomy stack to consumer vehicles. Headquartered in
Mountain View, CA (GM autonomous driving office).

## Technical Focus

- Level 4 autonomous driving for personal vehicles
- AI perception, prediction, and planning trained on 5M+ driverless
  miles from the Cruise robotaxi program
- Simulation framework for virtual AV testing at scale
- Integration of autonomy into GM's Super Cruise ADAS platform
- Eyes-off, hands-off driving system targeting 2028 launch (Cadillac
  Escalade IQ first)

## Scale & Stage

GM subsidiary (NYSE: GM). No longer a standalone company. GM is
rehiring former Cruise engineers at the Mountain View office. The
robotaxi shutdown saves GM $1B+ annually, with resources redirected
to personal vehicle autonomy. Led by Sterling Anderson (former Tesla
Autopilot chief).

## Hiring

Rehiring former Cruise engineers and recruiting new talent in Mountain
View. Hiring across perception, planning, simulation, and ML
engineering. Now embedded within GM's product development org rather
than operating as an independent unit.

Expect a transitional culture as the team shifts from robotaxi to
personal vehicle focus. GM provides scale and manufacturing expertise
but the autonomy team retains a Silicon Valley engineering identity.

## Recent Context

GM completed the Cruise merger in February 2025 and announced plans
for an eyes-off, hands-off driving system by 2028, starting with the
Cadillac Escalade IQ. The system will leverage Cruise's AI models and
simulation infrastructure. LiDAR-equipped fleet is logging miles
nationwide with safety drivers to build training data. Super Cruise
receiving Google Maps integration and other updates for 2026 model
year vehicles.
