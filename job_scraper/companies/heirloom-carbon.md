# Heirloom Carbon

## Overview

Heirloom Carbon Technologies builds direct air capture (DAC)
systems that use enhanced limestone mineralization to remove
CO2 from the atmosphere. Founded in 2020 by Shashank Samala
(CEO) and Noah McQueen (chief scientist, UPenn researcher).
Headquartered in San Francisco, CA.

## Technical Focus

- Enhanced limestone carbon mineralization for DAC
- Accelerated CO2 absorption (years compressed to days)
- Kiln-based calcination and CO2 concentration
- Permanent underground carbon storage partnerships
- Process engineering for cost reduction at scale

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B. Raised $150M Series B in Dec 2024
co-led by Future Positive and Lowercarbon Capital, with
Japan Airlines, Mitsubishi, Mitsui, and Siemens Financial
Services participating. Total funding ~$200M+. Approximately
50-100 employees. First commercial DAC plant operational in
Tracy, CA since late 2023.

## Hiring

Heirloom hires across mechanical engineering, chemical/process
engineering, systems engineering, and operations. The company
values candidates comfortable with hardware scale-up and
novel process development. Roles span R&D, plant operations,
and project engineering. Average hiring process is ~22 days.
Culture emphasizes urgency around climate impact and moving
from prototype to commercial scale.

## Recent Context

Opened North America's first commercial DAC facility in
Tracy, CA (2023). Announced a $475M investment for a second
facility in Louisiana, part of the DOE-backed Project Cypress
DAC Hub (eligible for up to $600M federal funding). The
Louisiana facility targets 17,000 tons/year initially,
scaling toward 1 million tons/year. Received investment from
Development Bank of Japan in late 2025. United Airlines'
Sustainable Flight Fund also invested.
