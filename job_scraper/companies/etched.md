# Etched

## Overview

AI chip startup building the world's first transformer-specific ASIC.
By hardcoding the transformer architecture directly into silicon, Etched
claims order-of-magnitude performance gains over general-purpose GPUs
for inference workloads on transformer models.

## Technical Focus

- Transformer-specialized ASIC design (Sohu chip)
- TSMC 4nm process
- High-throughput inference (500K+ tokens/sec on Llama-70B per 8-chip server)
- Text, image, and video transformer acceleration
- Purpose-built silicon vs. general-purpose GPU tradeoffs

## Scale & Stage

Private. Raised $500M at $5B valuation (2025-2026), total funding
~$620M. Founded 2022 in Menlo Park by Christopher Zhu and Gavin Uberti.
Early stage but very well capitalized. Backed by Peter Thiel and
Stripes.

## Hiring

Small, high-caliber team focused on chip design, verification, and
ML systems. The bet-the-company approach on transformers means they
look for candidates with deep conviction in the architecture's
longevity. Strong preference for ASIC design experience, particularly
at advanced process nodes.

Given the massive funding relative to team size, Etched is likely
scaling aggressively. Roles span silicon engineering, software for
chip toolchains, and ML performance optimization.

## Recent Context

Raised $500M at $5B valuation to push Sohu into data center production.
The Sohu chip on TSMC 4nm claims 20x+ throughput advantage over Nvidia
H100 for transformer inference. The core bet is that transformers
remain the dominant architecture — a risk if alternatives emerge.
