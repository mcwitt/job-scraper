# Lawrence Berkeley National Lab

## Overview

US Department of Energy national laboratory managed by UC Berkeley,
located in the hills above the Berkeley campus. World leader in
scientific computing, energy sciences, biological sciences, and
materials research. Home to NERSC, the DOE's mission HPC facility
serving 11,000+ scientists and 800+ research projects annually.

## Technical Focus

- High-performance computing: NERSC supercomputers (Perlmutter, upcoming Doudna)
- Scientific computing, data science, and computational research
- Energy sciences, materials science, and climate modeling
- ESnet: DOE's high-performance networking for science
- Quantum computing research and distributed quantum systems

## Scale & Stage

DOE national laboratory, publicly funded. ~3,800 employees (including
~800 students/postdocs). Annual budget ~$1.3-1.5B. Non-profit research
institution operated by the University of California.

## Hiring

Hires research scientists, engineers, postdocs, and students across
computing, physical sciences, and engineering. Strong pipeline from
UC Berkeley and top research universities. Positions typically require
domain expertise — HPC systems, scientific software, or specific
science disciplines. PhD preferred for research roles.

Government lab culture with academic freedom and mission-driven focus.
Compensation below industry but offers job stability, publication
opportunities, and access to world-class facilities. Summer research
programs for students in CS, data science, and mathematics.

## Recent Context

Announced NERSC-10 "Doudna" supercomputer (named after Nobel laureate
Jennifer Doudna), built on NVIDIA Vera-Rubin GPUs and Dell ORv3
liquid-cooled servers, delivering 10x Perlmutter performance when it
launches in 2026. Experiencing budget pressures and layoffs (~3% of
staff) due to DOE funding cuts in 2025.
