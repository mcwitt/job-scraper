# Helion Energy

## Overview

Pulsed fusion energy company building the world's first
fusion power plant. Founded in 2013 by David Kirtley (CEO),
Chris Pihl (CTO), John Slough, and George Votroubek.
Headquartered in Everett, Washington. Backed by Sam Altman.

## Technical Focus

- Pulsed non-ignition fusion (field-reversed configuration)
- Deuterium-tritium and deuterium-helium-3 fuel cycles
- Direct energy conversion from fusion plasma
- High-temperature superconducting magnets
- Plasma physics and power electronics

## Scale & Stage

Private. Over $1B total raised including $425M Series F
(Jan 2025). Valued at ~$5.4B post-money. ~500 employees
and growing. Pre-revenue; building first commercial plant.

## Hiring

Rapidly scaling engineering team. Roles span plasma
physics, power electronics, mechanical engineering, and
software. Hardware-heavy culture with urgency driven by
2028 commercial deadline. High technical bar with focus
on hands-on builders.

## Recent Context

Polaris prototype achieved 150M degree C plasma temps and
first private D-T fusion (Feb 2026). Broke ground on
Orion, a 50 MW commercial plant in Malaga, WA (Jul 2025)
with a power purchase agreement to supply Microsoft data
centers by 2028. Series F investors include Lightspeed,
SoftBank, Nucor.
