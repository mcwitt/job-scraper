# Emerald AI

## Overview

AI-powered energy flexibility platform that makes data center
power demand responsive to grid conditions. Software enables
data centers to dynamically adjust energy consumption by
orchestrating AI workloads across sites in real time. Founded
in 2024 by Dr. Varun Sivaram (CEO), a former Fortune 500
energy executive and senior U.S. diplomat. Headquartered in
Washington, DC (Clarendon offices).

## Technical Focus

- AI workload orchestration across data center networks
- Real-time power demand flexibility (Conductor platform)
- Grid stability and demand response optimization
- Distributed systems for multi-site compute scheduling
- Energy-aware AI training, fine-tuning, and inference

## Scale & Stage

Private, seed stage. $42.5M total seed funding: initial
$24.5M led by Radical Ventures (July 2025), then $18M
extension led by Lowercarbon Capital. Backed by NVIDIA
(NVentures), Kleiner Perkins' John Doerr, Google Chief
Scientist Jeff Dean, AI pioneer Fei-Fei Li, and former
Secretary of State John Kerry. Small team (~10-20), hiring
actively with 13 open roles.

## Hiring

Early-stage hiring where engineers shape the stack and
roadmap. Seeking backend/distributed systems engineers and
senior roles. Competitive pay ($135K-$187K for senior
backend). High-caliber team from hyperscalers (Amazon,
Intel), academia (BU), and energy industry. Culture of a
well-funded technical startup tackling climate + AI.

## Recent Context

Launched publicly in July 2025 with first commercial demo
at an Oracle data center in Arizona, cutting AI cluster
power by 25% during peak demand while maintaining compute
performance. Raised $18M seed extension in Oct 2025 with
NVIDIA and Lowercarbon. Positioned at the intersection of
AI scaling and grid constraints, bypassing 7-10 year
interconnection queues through software.
