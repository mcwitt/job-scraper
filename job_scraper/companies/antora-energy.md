# Antora Energy

## Overview

Thermal energy storage company delivering zero-carbon heat
and power for industrial decarbonization. Stores renewable
electricity as heat in solid carbon blocks at temperatures
above 1,800°C, then delivers on-demand process heat or
electricity via patented thermophotovoltaic (TPV) technology.
Founded in 2017 by Andrew Ponec. Headquartered in
Sunnyvale, California.

## Technical Focus

- Solid carbon thermal battery design and manufacturing
- Thermophotovoltaic (TPV) cells for heat-to-electricity
- Ultra-high-temperature thermal storage (1,500–1,800°C+)
- Industrial process heat delivery systems
- Manufacturing scale-up and module assembly
- Renewable energy integration and grid flexibility

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B ($150M led by Decarbonization Partners,
a BlackRock/Temasek joint venture). ~$309M total raised
including $71.7M debt round in Oct 2025. Valued at ~$500M.
~200+ employees. 50k sq ft manufacturing facility in San
Jose — world's largest TPV manufacturing operation.

## Hiring

Growing rapidly with 25+ open roles spanning electrical,
mechanical, and manufacturing engineering. Culture
emphasizes team and mission focus, humility, and openness.
Roles skew toward hardware: thermal systems, power
electronics, manufacturing process engineering, and
mechanical design. Strong fit for engineers with energy,
materials science, or power systems backgrounds.

## Recent Context

Commissioned first commercial-scale 5 MWh thermal battery
with Wellhead Electric near Fresno (2023). Selected by DOE
as partner on $215M grant to decarbonize Summit Materials
cement facilities. Named one of Fast Company's most
innovative companies of 2025. Scaling deployments to
chemicals, mining, food and beverage, and oil and gas
customers, with projects ramping through 2025–2026.
