# Lawrence Livermore National Lab

## Overview

US Department of Energy national laboratory focused
on national security, nuclear weapons stewardship,
and fundamental science. Co-founded by Edward Teller
and Ernest Lawrence in 1952. Located in Livermore,
California, about 40 miles east of San Francisco.
Managed by Lawrence Livermore National Security, LLC.

## Technical Focus

- Nuclear weapons stockpile stewardship and safety
- High-performance computing and simulation
- National Ignition Facility (NIF): world's largest
  laser system for fusion and weapons physics
- Cybersecurity, counterterrorism, and nonproliferation
- Climate and energy research

## Scale & Stage

Federally funded R&D center (FFRDC) under the DOE
National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA).
Over 9,000 employees. Main site spans 820 acres.
~85% of DOE budget allocation goes to nuclear
weapons-related activities.

## Hiring

Hires across scientific computing, physics,
engineering, cybersecurity, and operations. Strong
demand for HPC software engineers, computational
scientists, and data scientists. Requires US
citizenship and security clearance for many roles.
Ranked among Glassdoor's Best-Led Companies in 2025.

## Recent Context

El Capitan, built with HPE and AMD, became the
world's fastest supercomputer in November 2024 at
1.742 exaFLOPS, officially dedicated in February
2025. NIF continues fusion ignition experiments
following the landmark breakeven achievement in
December 2022.
