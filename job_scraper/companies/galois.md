# Galois

## Overview

Employee-owned R&D company specializing in formal methods, high-assurance
software, and cryptography. Builds tools and solutions that provide
mathematical guarantees of software correctness and security for defense,
aerospace, fintech, and healthcare applications. Founded in 1999,
headquartered in Portland, Oregon.

## Technical Focus

- Formal verification and formal methods (SAW, Cryptol)
- Cryptographic algorithm specification and verification
- High-assurance software development for critical systems
- AI for formal methods (making verification more accessible)
- Trusted computing and hardware/software co-design (DARPA COOP)

## Scale & Stage

Private, employee-owned. ~100-120 employees in Portland, OR. Revenue
primarily from government R&D contracts (DARPA, DoD) and commercial
consulting. Stable, mature organization with 25+ years of operation.

## Hiring

Deeply technical hiring bar focused on functional programming (Haskell,
Rust), formal verification, type theory, and cryptography. Strong
preference for candidates with academic research backgrounds or
demonstrated expertise in provably correct software. Publishes
open-source tools and values community contributions.

Culture is research-oriented with significant intellectual freedom.
Small teams work on high-impact problems where correctness is
non-negotiable. Not a typical startup environment -- more akin to an
applied research lab with long-term government and commercial
relationships.

## Recent Context

Awarded $12.6M DARPA contract for the COOP (Continuous-correctness On
Opaque Processors) program to develop trusted computing tools combining
formal methods with side-channel analysis (due April 2026). Actively
developing AI-assisted formal methods tools to make verification more
accessible to non-specialists.
