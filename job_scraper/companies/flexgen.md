# FlexGen

## Overview

Grid-scale battery energy storage and energy management
software company. Develops the HybridOS platform for
automating dispatch of storage, renewable, and conventional
generation assets. Founded in 2009 by Josh Prueher and Doug
Moorehead, originally building energy storage for the U.S.
military. Headquartered in Durham, North Carolina. Current
CEO is Kelcy Pegler Jr.

## Technical Focus

- HybridOS energy management system (hardware-agnostic EMS)
- Battery energy storage system (BESS) integration
- AI-enhanced predictive maintenance and optimization
- Real-time grid dispatch and demand response
- Multi-site fleet management and monitoring
- Solar + storage hybrid control systems

## Scale & Stage

Private. ~$275-360M total funding across 7 rounds, including
$75M debt round (Jan 2025). ~255-270 employees across 4
continents. Now managing 25+ GWh of storage across 200+
sites in 10 countries. Industry-leading 98% availability
rate. Significant scale increase through Powin acquisition.

## Hiring

Engineering roles across software (EMS platform), power
systems, and field operations. Military-origin culture with
emphasis on reliability and execution. Growing team after
Powin acquisition brought on former Powin engineers. Durham
HQ with distributed presence in North America, Asia, and
Europe.

## Recent Context

Completed acquisition of bankrupt competitor Powin's assets
and IP in August 2025, more than doubling managed fleet to
25+ GWh across 200+ sites. Launched HybridOS V12 with AI-
enhanced detection and action features. Released latest
HybridOS update (Feb 2026) unifying battery and solar
management. Positioned as a consolidator in the grid-scale
storage market.
