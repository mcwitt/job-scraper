# QuEra Computing

## Overview

Quantum computing company building neutral-atom quantum
computers. Spun out of research at Harvard and MIT, founded
in 2018 by Mikhail Lukin, Vladan Vuletic, Markus Greiner,
Dirk Englund, Nathan Gemelke, and John Pena. Headquartered
in Boston, Massachusetts. CEO Andy Ory (appointed 2024).

## Technical Focus

- Neutral-atom quantum computing hardware
- Fault-tolerant quantum error correction
- Scalable qubit architectures (demonstrated 448 qubits
  with Harvard/MIT/Yale)
- Quantum software and cloud access (Aquila platform)
- Applications in optimization, simulation, and ML

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised over $230M including a round led by Google
Quantum AI and SoftBank Vision Fund 2, with investment
from NVentures (NVIDIA). ~150 employees across three
continents. Roadmap targeting 10 to 100 logical qubits
by 2026-2027. Founded 2018.

## Hiring

Hires across quantum physics, hardware engineering,
software, and applied research. Roles demand deep
expertise in atomic/optical physics, quantum error
correction, or systems engineering. Strong academic
ties to Harvard and MIT research groups. Also hiring
software engineers for cloud platform and developer tools.

## Recent Context

Demonstrated fault-tolerant quantum computing milestones
in 2025 with Harvard, MIT, and Yale — continuous
operation, scalable error correction, and magic state
distillation. Partnering with NERSC for 2026 quantum
research access. Third-generation systems expected to
demonstrate in 2026-2027.
