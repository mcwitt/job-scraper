# Redwood Materials

## Overview

Redwood Materials, founded by former Tesla CTO JB Straubel, is the
dominant lithium-ion battery recycling company in North America. The
company recycles end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap to
recover critical materials (lithium, nickel, cobalt, copper) and
produce anode and cathode components for new batteries.

## Technical Focus

- Hydrometallurgical and pyrometallurgical battery recycling processes
- Anode copper foil and cathode active material production
- Battery energy storage system (BESS) deployment and operations
- Supply chain logistics for battery collection and processing

## Scale & Stage

Private, valued at $6B+ (October 2025 Series E). ~1,000+ employees.
Raised $425M Series E (closed January 2026) from Eclipse, Capricorn,
Goldman Sachs, NVentures (NVIDIA), and Google. Controls ~90% of
lithium-ion battery recycling in North America, processing equivalent
of 250,000 EV batteries/year. Operates facilities in Nevada and a new
$3.5B, 600-acre plant near Charleston, SC.

## Hiring

Redwood Materials is in rapid growth mode, hiring across chemical
engineering, process engineering, manufacturing operations, software,
and data roles. The company's expansion into battery energy storage
systems has broadened hiring needs beyond recycling into energy
markets and grid services.

JB Straubel's Tesla pedigree attracts strong talent, and the company
emphasizes mission-driven culture around circular economy and domestic
supply chain. The bar is high for technical roles, particularly in
electrochemistry and process engineering. Software roles focus on
manufacturing automation, logistics optimization, and energy trading.

## Recent Context

Redwood closed its Series E at $425M in January 2026 with Google
joining as a new investor, valuing the company at over $6B. The company
expanded into the battery energy storage market alongside its core
recycling business. Its new South Carolina plant represents a major
scale-up of domestic critical materials production capacity.
