# Charge Robotics

## Overview

Robotics company automating utility-scale solar farm
construction. Builds portable robotic factory systems
that assemble and install solar-tracking hardware on
site, reducing labor needs and accelerating build
timelines. Founded in 2021 by Banks Hunter and Max
Justicz (both MIT alumni). Headquartered in Oakland, CA.

## Technical Focus

- Autonomous solar construction robotics (Sunrise system)
- Fully automated assembly and installation of solar
  tracker hardware
- Portable factory systems deployable to job sites
- Computer vision and robotic manipulation for outdoor
  construction environments

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B. Raised $22M Series B in March 2025
($39M total across four rounds). Y Combinator alumnus.
Key investors include Climate Capital, E14 Fund,
FoundersX Ventures, and Hummingbird Ventures.

## Hiring

Hardware and software engineering roles focused on
robotics, mechanical design, computer vision, and
field deployment. Expect comfort with physical-world
systems and outdoor field work. Small, fast-moving
team with a climate-tech mission.

## Recent Context

Successfully deployed Sunrise, the company's fully
automated solar construction robotics system, in 2025.
Closed $22M Series B in March 2025 to scale production
and deployment. Featured in MIT News for making solar
projects cheaper and faster with portable factories.
