# Enode

## Overview

Enode provides a universal API that lets energy
companies connect to and control distributed energy
resources (DERs) including EVs, EV chargers, solar
inverters, home batteries, and thermostats. Founded
in 2020 by Henrik Langeland, Nikolai Heum, and
Thorvald Thorsnes, the company is headquartered in
Oslo, Norway. The API supports over 1,000 device
types across major hardware manufacturers.

## Technical Focus

- Unified REST API for DER device connectivity
- Real-time device telemetry and control
- EV smart charging and solar optimization
- Virtual power plant enablement for partners
- Cross-manufacturer device abstraction layer

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series A ($15M in Nov 2022) led by Creandum,
with Lowercarbon Capital, Y Combinator, BoxGroup, and
MCJ Collective. Total raised ~$17M across 4 rounds.
Approximately 50 employees as of 2026. Founded 2020.

## Hiring

Enode hires software engineers, developer relations,
and energy domain specialists. The team builds and
maintains integrations with hundreds of hardware APIs.
Engineering-heavy culture with a focus on API design
and reliability at scale.

## Recent Context

In 2025, Enode partnered with TotalEnergies to enable
consumers to connect solar inverters, HVAC, and other
devices into the TotalEnergies app. Enode also
partnered with Sense in August 2025 to scale EV energy
insights and controls. The company continues expanding
its device coverage as the DER market grows.
