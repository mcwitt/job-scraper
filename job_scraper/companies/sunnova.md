# Sunnova

## Overview

Sunnova was a major residential solar and energy services company that
provided solar panels, battery storage, and energy management through
lease and PPA financing models. The company filed for Chapter 11
bankruptcy in June 2025 and was subsequently acquired by Solaris
Assets LLC.

## Technical Focus

- Residential solar system design and deployment
- Battery storage and backup power systems
- Energy management and monitoring software
- Solar asset management and servicing platforms

## Scale & Stage

Formerly public (NYSE: NOVA). Filed Chapter 11 bankruptcy June 2025
with ~$8.9B in long-term debt. Acquired by Solaris Assets in September
2025. Operations transitioned to SunStrong Management LLC. Laid off 55%
of workforce in June 2025. Had served ~500,000 customers at peak.

## Hiring

Following the bankruptcy filing and acquisition, Sunnova's workforce
was significantly reduced with a 55% layoff in June 2025. The
go-forward entity under SunStrong Management focuses on solar asset
servicing and operations rather than new customer acquisition.

Any current hiring would be through SunStrong Management and focused on
asset management, customer service, and operational roles to maintain
the existing solar portfolio. The company's trajectory makes it a
higher-risk employer in the near term.

## Recent Context

Sunnova's collapse was driven by rising interest rates, California
policy changes (NEM 3.0), cancellation of a $3B DOE loan guarantee in
May 2025, and an industry-wide decline in residential solar
installations (31% drop in 2024). Solaris completed the acquisition in
September 2025, with SunStrong Management taking over operations to
ensure continuity for existing customers.
