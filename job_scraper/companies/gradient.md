# Gradient

## Overview

Gradient builds low-carbon window heat pumps designed
for easy installation without permanent building
modifications. Founded in 2017 by Vince Romanin and
Kipp Bradford, the company is headquartered in San
Francisco. Gradient's products use R32 refrigerant and
mount in windows without blocking the view, targeting
multifamily housing decarbonization.

## Technical Focus

- Window-mounted heat pump hardware (heating + cooling)
- Low-GWP refrigerant (R32) HVAC systems
- Gradient Nexus fleet management and control software
- 120V plug-in design for easy retrofit installation
- IoT-enabled energy optimization for building managers

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series A. Raised ~$50M total including $27.5M
Series A (2022-2023, led by Climate Investment). Also
received non-dilutive funding from U.S. DOE, California
Energy Commission, and California Strategic Growth
Council. Approximately 60 employees. Founded 2017.

## Hiring

Gradient hires across hardware engineering, mechanical
design, firmware, software, and operations. The company
needs talent in HVAC system design, embedded systems,
manufacturing scale-up, and building energy management.
As a hardware+software startup, individual contributors
work across the full product stack from R&D through
production.

## Recent Context

In February 2026, Gradient launched Nexus, an
intelligent energy control platform for managing fleets
of its 120V window heat pumps at scale in multifamily
buildings. New CEO Patrick Bischoff, a climate tech
veteran, was appointed in January 2025 to lead the
company through its next growth phase. The company
continues scaling production and expanding into
multifamily retrofit markets.
