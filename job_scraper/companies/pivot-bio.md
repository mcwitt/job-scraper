# Pivot Bio

## Overview

Pivot Bio is an agricultural biotechnology company that
develops microbial products to replace synthetic nitrogen
fertilizers for row crops. Founded in 2011 by Karsten
Temme and Cory Feala, headquartered in Berkeley, CA with
major operations in the St. Louis/Creve Coeur, MO area
and Minnetonka, MN. The company's flagship product uses
gene-edited naturally occurring microbes that colonize
plant roots and fix nitrogen directly, reducing farmers'
dependence on synthetic fertilizer.

## Technical Focus

- Microbiology and genomics-based nitrogen fixation
- Strain engineering and synthetic biology (gene editing)
- Fermentation, formulation, and downstream process
  development
- Agricultural field trials and agronomy science
- Precision agriculture data platform and analytics
- Manufacturing operations for biological ag inputs

## Scale & Stage

Private, late-stage. Has raised ~$600M+ in funding from
investors including Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Bill
Gates, Continental Grain, and others. The company has
launched commercial products (PROVEN series) and sells
to farmers across millions of acres in the U.S. Headcount
approximately 400–500 employees.

## Hiring

Pivot Bio hires across R&D (strain engineering,
fermentation, process development), commercial agronomy,
manufacturing/quality control, data/IT (Databricks-heavy
data platform), and corporate functions (finance, FP&A).
The Berkeley office hosts core science leadership; field
roles are distributed; operational and manufacturing
roles are primarily in the St. Louis metro area.

## Recent Context

Pivot Bio continues commercializing its PROVEN nitrogen
product line for corn and other row crops and expanding
market penetration with U.S. farmers. The company is
investing in data architecture (Databricks-based platform)
connecting lab discovery through manufacturing to field
performance, and building out its corporate finance
function to support board/investor reporting. The
company's sustainability program tracks grower nitrogen
management practices.
