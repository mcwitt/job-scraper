# NVIDIA

## Overview

Semiconductor company that designs GPUs and accelerators. Dominant
position in AI/ML training and inference hardware. Also strong in
gaming, autonomous vehicles (DRIVE), robotics, and scientific
computing.

## Technical Focus

- GPU architecture (CUDA, Tensor Cores)
- AI infrastructure: DGX systems, networking (Mellanox acquisition)
- Software platforms: CUDA toolkit, TensorRT, Triton, NeMo, Isaac
- Research: graphics, ML systems, robotics, autonomous driving

## Scale & Stage

Public (NASDAQ: NVDA). ~30,000 employees. Revenue driven primarily
by data center / AI segment. Rapid headcount growth in AI-related
roles.

## Hiring

Highly competitive. Strong preference for candidates with
relevant publications, deep systems experience, or demonstrated GPU/
accelerator expertise. Engineering bar is high — expect algorithm
and systems design depth. Known to hire from top academic programs
and peer companies (AMD, Intel, Google, Meta).

Adjacent backgrounds can succeed in platform/infra roles but core
GPU architecture and research roles typically require direct domain
experience.

## Recent Context

Dominant supplier of AI training hardware (H100/B200). Expanding
into inference, networking, and full-stack AI platforms. Significant
investment in custom silicon for hyperscalers and sovereign AI
initiatives.
