# Pano AI

## Overview

Pano AI provides early wildfire detection using a
combination of AI, ultra-high-definition cameras, and
satellite data. Founded in 2020 by Sonia Kastner (CEO)
and Arvind Satyam (CCO), the company is headquartered
in San Francisco. Pano deploys a network of 360-degree
cameras on high vantage points and uses computer vision
to detect smoke within minutes, providing real-time
situational awareness to fire agencies and utilities.

## Technical Focus

- Computer vision and AI for smoke/fire detection
- Ultra-HD 360-degree camera hardware and deployment
- Satellite imagery integration and analysis
- Real-time alerting and situational awareness platform
- Geospatial data processing and visualization
- Cloud infrastructure (Cloudflare, Nginx, TypeScript)

## Scale & Stage

Private. Raised $89M total through Series B ($44M
Series B in June 2025 led by Giant Ventures, with
Liberty Mutual Strategic Ventures, Tokio Marine Future
Fund, Congruent Ventures, Initialized Capital, and
Salesforce Ventures). Approximately 150-200 employees
across North America, Australia, and Asia. Named to
TIME's 100 Most Influential Companies of 2025.

## Hiring

Pano hires across AI/ML, computer vision, hardware
engineering, software development, and operations.
The team draws from companies like Cisco, Tesla, Apple,
Salesforce, and Nest. Mission-driven culture focused on
wildfire prevention and climate resilience. Technical
roles often require experience with real-time systems,
edge computing, or geospatial data pipelines.

## Recent Context

In February 2026, Pano announced a strategic
partnership with Technosylva to combine predictive
wildfire risk analysis with live camera-based ignition
detection. The company serves over 250 first responder
agencies across 10 U.S. states, five Australian states,
and British Columbia, and is trusted by 15 U.S. electric
utilities including Arizona Public Service, Portland
General Electric, and Xcel Energy.
