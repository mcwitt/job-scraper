# Google

## Overview

Alphabet subsidiary and the world's dominant search engine, advertising
platform, and cloud provider. Operates YouTube, Android, Chrome, and a
broad portfolio of AI/ML products and services. Increasingly an
AI-first company with Gemini models embedded across all products.

## Technical Focus

- AI/ML: Gemini models, DeepMind research, AI infrastructure (TPUs, Ironwood)
- Cloud: Google Cloud Platform, Kubernetes, BigQuery, Spanner
- Search, ads, and recommendation systems at massive scale
- YouTube streaming and content infrastructure
- Android, Chrome, and developer platforms

## Scale & Stage

Public (NASDAQ: GOOGL). ~190,000 employees, ~80,000 in engineering.
FY2025 revenue exceeded $400B. Google Cloud revenue $17.7B in Q4 2025
alone (up 48% YoY). Planning $175-185B capex in 2026, nearly double
2025, primarily for AI infrastructure.

## Hiring

Extremely selective — less than 0.2% acceptance rate for engineering
roles. Interview process is 2-3 technical rounds plus behavioral
("Googleyness & Leadership"). Strong emphasis on algorithms, system
design, and collaborative personality. Language-agnostic but expects
elegant, readable code. Now hires new grads and experienced alike.

Engineering-first culture with high autonomy. In 2025, 20% of AI
software engineers hired were boomerang employees. Voluntary buyouts
offered across divisions as part of restructuring, but AI-related
headcount continues to grow rapidly.

## Recent Context

Released Gemini 3, reaching 8 million paying enterprise licenses.
Announced Ironwood TPUs with 10x performance over prior generation.
Shifting to multi-agent architectures for enterprise AI and partnering
with DOE national labs via AI co-scientist program.
