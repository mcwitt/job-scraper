# UK AI Security Institute

## Overview

UK government research body focused on understanding and
mitigating risks from advanced AI. Operates within the
Department for Science, Innovation and Technology. Originally
established as the Frontier AI Taskforce in April 2023,
became the AI Safety Institute after the Bletchley Park AI
Safety Summit in November 2023, and renamed to AI Security
Institute in February 2025. Chaired by Ian Hogarth.
Based in London.

## Technical Focus

- Pre-release safety evaluation of frontier AI models
- AI alignment research (The Alignment Project)
- Capability and risk assessment of advanced AI systems
- Mitigations development for AI-related threats
- Policy-relevant technical research

## Scale & Stage

Government body with over £100M initial budget. 100+
technical staff drawn from leading AI labs, academia,
and nonprofits. Interim Director Adam Beaumont (ex-GCHQ
AI chief). Chief Scientist Geoffrey Irving (ex-OpenAI,
DeepMind). Over £15M distributed in grant funding.

## Hiring

Hires researchers, engineers, and policy specialists.
Technical roles emphasize ML safety evaluation, red-teaming,
and alignment research. Strong hires from DeepMind, OpenAI,
and top UK universities. Government pay scales but
mission-driven culture attracts top AI safety talent.

## Recent Context

Published inaugural Frontier AI Trends Report drawing on
two years of model evaluations. Piloted AI productivity
studies with the Future of Work Unit. The Alignment Project
applications reopening summer 2026. Actively collaborating
with leading AI companies on pre-release safety testing.
