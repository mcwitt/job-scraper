# Clearway Energy

## Overview

Clearway Energy is one of the largest owners of clean
energy generation assets in the U.S., with approximately
12.7 GW of gross capacity across 27 states — including
9.9 GW of wind, solar, and storage and 2.8 GW of
dispatchable gas generation. Headquartered in San
Francisco, the company trades on the NYSE (CWEN). Owned
by Global Infrastructure Partners and TotalEnergies.

## Technical Focus

- Utility-scale wind, solar, and energy storage
- Dispatchable natural gas generation for grid
  reliability
- Data center power purchase agreements
- Renewable project development (27 GW pipeline)
- Paired and standalone battery storage

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: CWEN/CWEN.A). Approximately 760 employees.
2025 full-year Adjusted EBITDA of $1.2B and CAFD of
$430M. 27 GW development pipeline including 8.2 GW
solar, 8 GW standalone storage, 4.6 GW wind, and 2.6 GW
gas for data centers. Founded 2016.

## Hiring

Clearway hires across renewable energy development,
project finance, asset management, grid operations, and
engineering. As a large public IPP, roles span the full
lifecycle from development through long-term operations.
Experience in power markets, utility-scale renewables,
and energy finance is valued.

## Recent Context

In January 2026, Clearway announced three long-term PPAs
with Google totaling ~1.1 GW across Missouri, Texas, and
West Virginia. The company executed 1.8 GW of data
center PPAs in 2025 and is developing gigawatt-class
co-located data center generation across five states.
Reaffirmed 2026 CAFD guidance of $470M-$510M.
