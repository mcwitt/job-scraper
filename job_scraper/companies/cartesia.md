# Cartesia

## Overview

AI research company building efficient foundation models based on state
space models (SSMs), a novel architecture invented by the founding team at
Stanford AI Lab. Focused on real-time AI applications, particularly voice,
where SSMs enable lower latency and better long-term memory than
transformers.

## Technical Focus

- State space model (SSM) architecture research and development
- Real-time voice AI (Sonic 2.0: 90ms latency, 40ms turbo)
- Efficient inference for edge and real-time applications
- Foundation model training with sub-transformer compute requirements

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B. Raised $100M Series B in October 2025 ($191M total).
Series A ($64M) led by Kleiner Perkins in March 2025. ~87 employees.
Founded by Stanford PhDs who originated state space models.

## Hiring

Research-heavy organization with deep expertise in sequence modeling,
efficient architectures, and audio/speech ML. Hiring primarily researchers
and engineers with experience in novel model architectures, efficient
inference, audio/speech processing, or systems optimization for real-time
workloads.

Small team with strong academic pedigree (Stanford AI Lab origins). Expect a
high research bar and emphasis on architectural innovation rather than
scaling existing approaches. Growing quickly post-Series B.

## Recent Context

Launched Sonic 2.0, built on their latest SSM architecture -- twice the size
of the original Sonic but faster, achieving 90ms full latency and 40ms
turbo. Raised $191M total across two rounds in 2025, establishing SSMs as a
credible alternative to transformers for latency-sensitive workloads.
