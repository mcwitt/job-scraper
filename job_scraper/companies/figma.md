# Figma

## Overview

Collaborative design platform enabling teams to design,
prototype, and build products together in real time via
the browser. Founded in 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan
Wallace (Brown University CS grads). Headquartered in
San Francisco.

## Technical Focus

- Browser-based real-time collaborative design engine
  (WebGL, WebAssembly, CRDT-based multiplayer)
- AI-powered design tools (Figma Make, AI image editing,
  object removal, background extension)
- Website builder (Figma Sites) with CMS in public beta
- Vector editing and illustration (Figma Draw)
- Marketing asset creation (Figma Buzz)
- OpenAI Codex and Anthropic Claude Code integrations
  via MCP server

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: FIG). IPO July 2025 at $33/share, raised
~$1.2B. ~2,800 employees as of early 2026. Previously
raised $749M over 7 rounds including a $416M Series F
in May 2024. Adobe attempted a $20B acquisition in
2022, abandoned in 2023 due to regulatory concerns.

## Hiring

Engineering is the largest department (~755 people,
~40% of staff). Strong culture around performance
optimization, rethinking application architecture, and
cross-functional collaboration. Values curiosity and
breadth over narrow specialization. Active early-career
and intern programs. ~38 open roles as of March 2026.

## Recent Context

Went public in July 2025; stock briefly hit $143 before
settling lower. Acquired Israeli AI startup Weavy for
~$200M (October 2025), rebranded as Figma Weave for
AI image/video editing. Launched Figma Make (AI-powered
prototyping with Gemini), Figma Sites, Figma Draw, and
Figma Buzz at Config 2025. Partnered with OpenAI and
Anthropic for AI coding integrations in early 2026.
