# X, the moonshot factory

## Overview

Alphabet's moonshot lab, pursuing radical 10x solutions
to the world's biggest problems. Founded in 2010 by
Sergey Brin, Sebastian Thrun, and Astro Teller. HQ in
Mountain View, CA. Astro Teller serves as "Captain of
Moonshots." Known for spinning out successful projects
as independent companies (Waymo, Wing, Verily, Loon,
Chronicle).

## Technical Focus

- AI/ML for Earth observation and disaster prediction
- Electric grid modernization and energy systems
- Wireless optical communications (Taara)
- Agricultural biotech and crop breeding (Heritable)
- AI for architecture and urban planning (Anori)
- Circular economy and advanced recycling
- Rapid prototyping and hardware R&D
- Robotics and autonomous systems

## Scale & Stage

Subsidiary of Alphabet (public, GOOGL). ~2,000+
employees. Operates as an internal R&D lab with its
own budget. Launched Series X Capital fund ($500M+,
led by Gideon Yu) to spin out mature projects as
independent companies with external funding.

## Hiring

Alphabet-level hiring bar. Roles span AI/ML, hardware
engineering, science, product, and operations. Culture
emphasizes rapid evaluation — projects are expected to
fail fast or graduate. Interdisciplinary teams mixing
scientists, engineers, and designers. Compensation
competitive with Google/Alphabet. Relatively small
headcount means selective hiring.

## Recent Context

Shifted strategy toward spinning out projects as
independent companies via Series X Capital. Active
moonshots include Tapestry (AI for electric grids,
partnering with PJM Interconnect), Bellwether (AI
disaster forecasting, named TIME Best Invention 2024),
and Moonshot for Circularity (plastics recycling).
Spun out Taara and Heritable Agriculture in 2025.
Launched Anori for AI-powered building development.
