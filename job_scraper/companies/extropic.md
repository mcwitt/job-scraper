# Extropic

## Overview

AI hardware startup building thermodynamic computing chips that run
probabilistic AI algorithms as a native physics process. Founded by
former Alphabet X quantum computing researchers, Extropic uses thermal
noise in standard CMOS circuits to perform generative AI inference
with radical energy efficiency.

## Technical Focus

- Probabilistic circuits using thermal noise for computation
- All-transistor thermodynamic chip design (standard CMOS)
- THRML software library for thermodynamic algorithms
- Diffusion Thermodynamic Models (DTM)
- Hardware-software co-design for energy-efficient generative AI

## Scale & Stage

Private. Seed round of $14.1M (Dec 2023) led by Kindred Ventures.
~25 employees. Founded by Guillaume Verdon (ex-Alphabet X quantum
lead), CTO Trevor McCourt, and Christopher Chamberland (ex-AWS/IBM/
Microsoft). Early stage, moving from prototypes to production chips.

## Hiring

Extremely selective, research-heavy team. Backgrounds in quantum
computing, physics, analog circuit design, and probabilistic ML are
highly valued. The founding team has deep academic credentials
(University of Waterloo) and industry experience at Alphabet, AWS,
IBM, and Microsoft.

Small team size means every hire matters significantly. Expect
interview process to probe deep understanding of thermodynamics,
stochastic processes, and hardware-algorithm co-design.

## Recent Context

Shipped X0 silicon prototype (Q1 2025) validating physics of
all-transistor probabilistic circuits. XTR-0 dev platform launched
Q3 2025 for external researchers. Z1 production chip with hundreds
of thousands of probabilistic circuits entering early access in 2026,
manufactured on standard CMOS.
