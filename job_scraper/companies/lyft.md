# Lyft

## Overview

Ridesharing platform operating across the US and Canada, offering
rides, bikes, scooters, and transit integrations. Second-largest
US rideshare company behind Uber. Increasingly positioning as the
platform layer for autonomous vehicle deployment, partnering with
multiple AV companies rather than building its own self-driving
technology.

## Technical Focus

- Real-time marketplace optimization (pricing, matching, routing,
  demand prediction)
- Autonomous vehicle fleet management and integration platform
- ML for ETA prediction, fraud detection, and safety
- Mobile engineering (iOS/Swift, Android)
- Large-scale data infrastructure and analytics
- Fleet management systems (Flexdrive subsidiary)

## Scale & Stage

Public (NASDAQ: LYFT). ~3,900 employees. 2025 revenue of ~$6.3B,
growing ~9% YoY. Net income of $2.8B in 2025 (up from $23M in
2024, likely including one-time gains). Free cash flow of $1.1B.
Operationally profitable and generating significant cash.

## Hiring

Engineering roles span marketplace algorithms, ML, mobile, data
engineering, and security infrastructure. Opening Toronto tech hub
with 60+ positions. Culture rated highly for work-life balance and
inclusion. Intentionally small intern cohorts with dedicated
mentorship. Expect strong systems and ML backgrounds for core
platform roles.

## Recent Context

Declaring 2026 the "Year of the AV" with multiple autonomous
vehicle partnerships: Tensor (Nvidia-powered robocars for 2026-27),
Baidu Apollo Go (Europe deployments pending regulatory approval),
BENTELER/Holon (autonomous shuttles in 2026), and May Mobility
(Atlanta deployments). Strategy is platform-level AV integration
rather than building proprietary self-driving tech.
