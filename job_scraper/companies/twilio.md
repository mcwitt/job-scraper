# Twilio

## Overview

Cloud communications platform (CPaaS) providing APIs for
voice, messaging, video, email, and authentication. Founded
in 2008 by Jeff Lawson, Evan Cooke, and John Wolthuis.
Headquartered in San Francisco, CA. Powers customer
engagement for millions of developers and enterprises
worldwide via programmable communication primitives.

## Technical Focus

- Programmable voice, SMS/MMS, WhatsApp, and RCS APIs
- Twilio Segment customer data platform (CDP)
- ConversationRelay for building voice AI agents with
  any LLM, streaming, and interruption handling
- Conversational Intelligence for analyzing calls and
  messages into structured data
- Twilio Flex cloud contact center platform
- SendGrid email delivery and marketing platform
- Identity and verification APIs (Verify, Lookup)

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: TWLO). ~6,600 employees. Full-year 2025
revenue $5.07B (+14% YoY reported, +13% organic). Guiding
2026 revenue growth of 11.5%-12.5%. Non-GAAP operating
income guided at $1.04B-$1.06B for 2026. Free cash flow
positive.

## Hiring

Remote-first culture with ~1,000 open roles as of early
2026. Engineering interviews average ~25 days, rated
moderate difficulty. Strong intern pipeline with 12-week
summer program and ~70-80% conversion rate. Values
diverse backgrounds and non-traditional career paths.
Collaborative, developer-centric culture.

## Recent Context

Announced multi-year strategic partnership with Microsoft
to accelerate conversational AI adoption (SIGNAL 2025).
Launched ConversationRelay (GA) for voice AI agents and
RCS messaging (GA) for branded business communications.
Added Conversational Intelligence for voice and messaging
analytics. Partnered with AEG to deploy across Crypto.com
Arena, LA Kings, and AXS ticketing.
