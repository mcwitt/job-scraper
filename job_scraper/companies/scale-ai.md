# Scale AI

## Overview

Data-centric AI infrastructure company providing data labeling,
RLHF, model evaluation, and generative AI tooling. Founded in
2016 by Alexandr Wang and Lucy Guo out of Y Combinator. HQ in
San Francisco. Clients span frontier AI labs, enterprises, and
the U.S. government. Operates Remotasks (computer vision /
autonomous vehicles) and Outlier (LLM annotation) subsidiaries.

## Technical Focus

- Data labeling and annotation pipelines at scale
- Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)
- LLM evaluation and benchmarking (co-released
  Humanity's Last Exam with CAIS, January 2025)
- Data Engine: end-to-end data collection, curation,
  annotation, and model evaluation
- Autonomous vehicle perception data
- Government / defense AI (Thunderforge project for DoD)

## Scale & Stage

Private. Series G ($14.3B from Meta for 49% stake, June
2025) valued the company at ~$29B. Total funding ~$15.9B
over 9 rounds. ~900 employees. Revenue ~$870M in 2024 with
a $1.5B annualized run rate by year-end; projected to exceed
$2B in 2025. Named to CNBC Disruptor 50 list in 2025.

## Hiring

Engineering-heavy org hiring across ML, data platform, and
product roles (~400 open positions as of early 2026). Known
for a fast-paced, high-intensity culture. Final-round
interviews run 3-4 loops in a single day including a
behavioral assessment for values fit. Flexible work hours
and remote-friendly benefits.

## Recent Context

Meta acquired a 49% stake for $14.3B in June 2025; founder
Alexandr Wang departed to lead Meta's Superintelligence Lab
and Jason Droege (former Uber exec) became interim CEO. The
Meta deal triggered customer departures from OpenAI, Google,
and xAI. Signed a five-year AI partnership with Qatar
(February 2025) and a $249M DoD contract for the Thunderforge
defense AI project (March 2025).
