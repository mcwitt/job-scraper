# Groq

## Overview

AI inference chip company that designs custom LPU (Language Processing Unit)
accelerators optimized for low-latency, high-throughput inference workloads.
Founded by former Google TPU engineer Jonathan Ross, Groq builds both the
silicon and the GroqCloud platform for serving LLM inference at scale.

## Technical Focus

- Custom LPU ASIC architecture for deterministic, low-latency inference
- GroqCloud inference platform (API-based LLM serving)
- Compiler and runtime for LPU hardware
- Data center infrastructure and deployment at global scale

## Scale & Stage

Private (acquired by Nvidia in late 2025). Raised $750M in September 2025 at
$6.9B valuation. ~590 employees across 6 continents. Projected $500M revenue
in 2025. Powered 2M+ developers and Fortune 500 customers.

## Hiring

Engineering-heavy organization focused on chip design, compiler engineering,
and systems software. High bar for hardware architects, compiler engineers,
and ML infrastructure roles. Experience with custom accelerator design, HPC,
or high-performance compiler toolchains is strongly valued.

Cloud platform and developer experience roles also available. The company
grew rapidly through 2025, expanding from North America into Europe and the
Middle East with new data center buildouts.

## Recent Context

In December 2025, Groq entered a non-exclusive licensing agreement with
Nvidia structured as $17B in cash payments, with Groq's CEO and senior
leaders joining Nvidia while Groq continues as an independent company.
Earlier in 2025, Groq secured a $1.5B commitment from Saudi Arabia for a
GroqCloud data center in Dammam.
