# Pachama

## Overview

Pachama built an AI-powered platform for measuring,
verifying, and monitoring forest carbon projects using
satellite imagery and machine learning. Founded in 2018
by Diego Saez Gil and Tomas Aftalion, the company was
headquartered in San Francisco. In November 2025,
Pachama was acquired by Carbon Direct to create an
integrated carbon management platform.

## Technical Focus

- Satellite imagery analysis for forest carbon
  measurement
- AI-driven monitoring, reporting, and verification
  (MRV) for carbon credits
- Remote forest biomass and carbon stock estimation
- Carbon credit quality assessment and validation
- Integration with voluntary carbon market standards

## Scale & Stage

Acquired by Carbon Direct (Nov 2025). Previously
raised $88M total through multiple venture rounds
including backing from Y Combinator, Breakthrough
Energy Ventures, and others. Approximately 40-50
employees at time of acquisition. Co-founder Diego
Saez Gil joined Carbon Direct as SVP Strategic
Engagement. Founded 2018.

## Hiring

As part of Carbon Direct, Pachama's team works on
forest carbon technology, machine learning, remote
sensing, and carbon market operations. Roles require
expertise in satellite imagery processing, forest
ecology, carbon accounting standards, and ML model
development. The acquisition expanded the combined
team's scope across the full carbon credit lifecycle.

## Recent Context

Carbon Direct acquired Pachama in November 2025,
combining Pachama's digital MRV platform with Carbon
Direct's science-centric advisory business. The merger
addresses weak verification and data integrity issues
in voluntary carbon markets. Verra, the world's
largest carbon credit program, has piloted Pachama's
digital measurement technology for forest carbon.
