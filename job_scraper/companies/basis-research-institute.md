# Basis Research Institute

## Overview

Nonprofit AI research organization (501(c)(3)) focused
on probabilistic programming, causal reasoning, and
building open-source tools for scientific modeling and
decision-making. Founded in 2022 by Eli Bingham,
Emily Mackevicius, and Zenna Tavares. Based in
New York, NY and Cambridge, MA.

## Technical Focus

- Probabilistic programming languages (Pyro, NumPyro)
- ChiRho: causal reasoning extension for Pyro
- Bayesian inference and causal modeling
- Open-source scientific computing tools
- Collaborative intelligent systems research

## Scale & Stage

Nonprofit, 501(c)(3) public charity. Small research
team (~15-30 staff). Co-founders include core
developers of Pyro (Uber/Meta) with deep ties to
the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. Funded
through grants and philanthropic contributions.

## Hiring

Research scientist roles in probabilistic programming,
compilers, programming languages, and causal inference.
Extremely high academic bar -- team members typically
hold PhDs and have published in top ML/PL venues.
Culture blends academic research rigor with open-source
software engineering.

## Recent Context

Actively developing ChiRho as a causal reasoning
framework layered on top of Pyro. Hiring research
scientists for compilers and programming languages
work. Positioned at the intersection of AI safety,
scientific modeling, and public-interest research.
