# Modular

## Overview

AI developer platform company building MAX (Modular Accelerated Xecution)
and the Mojo programming language. Founded by Chris Lattner (creator of LLVM
and Swift) and Tim Davis. Focused on unifying AI compute across hardware
backends with a high-performance, developer-friendly stack.

## Technical Focus

- Mojo language: Python-superset systems language for AI/HPC workloads
- MAX platform: unified inference engine across CPU, GPU, and accelerators
- Compiler infrastructure (MLIR-based) for AI model optimization
- Hardware abstraction layer for portable AI deployment

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series C. Raised $250M in September 2025 at $1.6B valuation ($380M
total). ~310 employees. Backed by DFJ Growth, GV, General Catalyst, and
Greylock.

## Hiring

Deep compiler and systems engineering culture reflecting the founders'
backgrounds. Strong preference for candidates with experience in compilers,
MLIR/LLVM, programming language design, GPU kernels, or high-performance
runtime systems. The team skews heavily toward systems programmers.

Also hiring across AI infrastructure, developer experience, and go-to-market
as they expand from inference into training workloads. Expect a high
technical bar and emphasis on first-principles thinking.

## Recent Context

Mojo is on track for 1.0 release in H1 2026, which will also open-source the
compiler. MAX Platform 26.1 (January 2026) graduated the Python API out of
experimental with PyTorch-like eager mode and model.compile() for production.
Expanding from inference into AI training with the new funding.
