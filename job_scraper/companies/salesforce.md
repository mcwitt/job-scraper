# Salesforce

## Overview

Enterprise cloud platform and the world's largest CRM provider,
serving over 150,000 companies including 90%+ of the Fortune
500. Founded in 1999 by Marc Benioff, Parker Harris, Dave
Moellenhoff, and Frank Dominguez. Headquartered in San
Francisco (Salesforce Tower). Pioneered the SaaS model for
enterprise software.

## Technical Focus

- CRM platform (Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud)
- Agentic AI platform (Agentforce) for autonomous AI agents
- Data Cloud and AI-powered analytics (Tableau, Einstein)
- Enterprise data management and governance (Informatica)
- Integration and API management (MuleSoft)
- Low-code app development (Salesforce Platform, Heroku)
- Industry-specific cloud solutions (Financial Services,
  Health, Communications)

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: CRM). ~76,000 employees. FY2026 revenue
~$41.5B, up ~10% YoY. Subscription and support is ~94% of
revenue. Largest CRM vendor globally with ~21% market share.
Completed $8B acquisition of Informatica in November 2025 to
strengthen data foundations for agentic AI.

## Hiring

Shifted hiring emphasis toward AI, data engineering, and
sales roles. Benioff stated in 2025 that AI productivity
gains reduced need for new software engineers, while adding
1,000-2,000 salespeople. Prioritizes specialists in AI, Data
Cloud, and multi-cloud integration. Strong internship program
(Futureforce). Culture emphasizes high performance, continuous
learning, and the 1-1-1 philanthropy model.

## Recent Context

Agentforce platform is the central product bet — launched
Agentforce Contact Center (native CCaaS with AI agents) and
Agentforce for Communications with industry-specific agents
in early 2026. Completed Informatica acquisition ahead of
schedule (November 2025) to unify data integration and
governance for agentic AI workloads. Spring 2026 release
expanded AI agent capabilities across the platform.
