# EnergyHub

## Overview

EnergyHub builds a distributed energy resource management (DERMS)
platform that enables utilities to manage and orchestrate residential
DERs -- thermostats, EVs, batteries, water heaters -- as virtual power
plants. The company operates one of North America's largest cross-DER
VPP platforms, working with over 120 utilities.

## Technical Focus

- Edge DERMS platform for cross-DER virtual power plant orchestration
- Device integration across thermostats, EVs, batteries, and other DERs
- Demand response aggregation and program management
- Grid analytics, forecasting, and telemetry for VPP dispatch

## Scale & Stage

Independent subsidiary of Alarm.com (Nasdaq: ALRM), acquired in 2013.
~180-200 employees. Manages 2.5+ GW of dispatchable flexible capacity.
Seven utility clients each manage 100,000+ enrolled devices on the
platform. Revenue not separately reported from Alarm.com.

## Hiring

EnergyHub hires engineers who are motivated by climate impact and
comfortable working at the intersection of energy systems and software.
Roles span backend engineering, data engineering, device integrations,
and product. The team is relatively small, so engineers tend to have
broad ownership and direct impact.

As a subsidiary of Alarm.com, EnergyHub offers more stability than a
typical startup while maintaining a smaller-team feel. Candidates with
experience in energy, IoT, or distributed systems are well-positioned,
though the company also values strong generalist engineers.

## Recent Context

In 2025, EnergyHub acquired Bridge to Renewables (EV OEM integration)
and Resideo Grid Services (demand response aggregation), consolidating
its position as a single-platform DERMS provider. The company's 2026
focus is on proving VPPs can perform like conventional generation
resources with year-round availability and operator-defined scheduling.
