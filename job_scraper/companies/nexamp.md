# Nexamp

## Overview

Nexamp is the largest community solar developer and
owner in the U.S., with end-to-end capabilities spanning
project development, design, construction, operations,
and asset management. Headquartered in Boston, MA, the
company was founded in 2007 and is majority owned by
Mitsubishi Corporation through Diamond Generating Corp.
CEO Zaid Ashai leads the company.

## Technical Focus

- Community solar project development and construction
- Distributed solar and battery energy storage
- Utility-scale solar and storage pipeline (6 GW)
- Subscriber management and billing platforms
- Solar asset operations and maintenance

## Scale & Stage

Private, majority owned by Mitsubishi Corporation.
Approximately 500 employees. Raised $520M capital raise
led by Manulife in 2024. Secured $340M refinancing (Apr
2025), $350M from Macquarie (Sep 2025), and $330M
revolving facility (Oct 2025). Targeting 3 GW community
solar by 2028, representing ~$3.9B investment.
Founded 2007.

## Hiring

Nexamp hires across solar project development,
engineering, construction, finance, and subscriber
operations. The company's growth into utility-scale
solar and storage means expanding demand for power
markets, grid interconnection, and battery storage
expertise. Strong culture of mission-driven clean
energy development.

## Recent Context

In 2025, Nexamp secured over $1B in financing across
multiple deals to fund its aggressive growth. Mitsubishi
announced a $3.9B investment plan for U.S. community
solar through Nexamp. The company is expanding from its
community solar roots into utility-scale solar and
storage, with a 6 GW pipeline under development.
