# VEIR

## Overview

VEIR develops high-temperature superconductor (HTS)
transmission lines that carry 5-10x more power along the
same right-of-way as conventional lines. The company's
proprietary evaporative cooling architecture delivers 20x
the cooling power per kilogram of coolant compared to
traditional approaches. Founded in 2019, VEIR is
headquartered in Woburn, MA. Seed-funded by Breakthrough
Energy Ventures.

## Technical Focus

- High-temperature superconductor (HTS) power lines
- Evaporative liquid nitrogen cooling systems
- AC overhead transmission (up to 400 MW, 69 kV)
- Low-voltage DC cable systems for data centers (3 MW)
- Grid interconnection and power delivery engineering
- Hardware prototyping and field deployment

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised $117M total including a $75M Series B
(Jan 2025, led by Munich Re Ventures) with participation
from Microsoft Climate Innovation Fund, Piva Capital,
National Grid Partners, and Dara Holdings. Growing team
with recent CPO hire (Jan 2026).

## Hiring

VEIR hires electrical engineers, power systems engineers,
cryogenics specialists, mechanical engineers, and product
managers. The company operates at the intersection of
materials science, power engineering, and hardware
deployment. As an early-stage deep-tech company backed by
Breakthrough Energy and Microsoft, individual contributors
shape the product direction significantly.

## Recent Context

VEIR closed an oversubscribed $75M Series B in Jan 2025 to
commercialize superconducting solutions for data centers,
utilities, and renewable developers. Microsoft is backing
VEIR to bring superconductors to its data centers, with a
proof-of-concept deployment planned. The company appointed
a Chief Product Officer in Jan 2026.
