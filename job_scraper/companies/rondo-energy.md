# Rondo Energy

## Overview

Industrial decarbonization company building thermal energy
storage systems (heat batteries) that convert intermittent
renewable electricity into continuous high-temperature
industrial heat. Founded by John O'Donnell and Pete Von
Behrens. Headquartered in Alameda, California. CEO Eric
Trusiewicz (appointed 2025, succeeding co-founder
O'Donnell who became chief innovation officer).

## Technical Focus

- Thermal energy storage using refractory brick heated
  to 1100-1500C
- Heat battery system design delivering superheated air
  at 98%+ efficiency
- Gigafactory-scale manufacturing (90 GWh ramp target)
- Integration with industrial processes across sectors
- Multi-hour to multi-day heat storage and dispatch

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised ~$195M total including Series B (June
2024). ~150 employees. First 100 MWh commercial system
operational in Kern County, California. Eleven commercial
developments announced across five industries. Three
projects in development in Europe. Founded ~2020.

## Hiring

Hires mechanical, thermal, and manufacturing engineers
plus project developers and operations staff. Values
experience in industrial process heat, energy storage,
or large-scale hardware deployment. Growing from pilot
to commercial scale, so roles span R&D, manufacturing
ramp-up, and project delivery.

## Recent Context

First major 100 MWh heat battery operational at Holmes
Western Oil Corp. in California (2025). Deployed
Southeast Asia's first industrial heat battery with SCG
Cleanergy at a cement plant in Thailand. Expanding into
European markets. Pursuing aggressive manufacturing
scale-up at heat battery gigafactory.
