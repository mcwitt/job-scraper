# Normal Computing

## Overview

Startup pioneering thermodynamic computing — a fundamentally new
computing paradigm that harnesses thermal noise and physical dynamics
(fluctuations, dissipation, stochasticity) to perform computation,
rather than fighting them as traditional semiconductors do.

## Technical Focus

- Thermodynamic semiconductor design (CN101 chip)
- Physics-based ASICs for linear algebra and matrix operations
- Stochastic sampling via lattice random walk
- Energy-efficient AI training and inference
- Claims up to 1,000x energy efficiency improvement

## Scale & Stage

Private. Raised ~$25M total. Small team (~45 employees). Pre-revenue
R&D stage with first chip (CN101) taped out in August 2025. Roadmap
extends through CN301 in late 2027/early 2028.

## Hiring

Deep technical hiring at the intersection of physics, electrical
engineering, and computer science. The thermodynamic computing paradigm
is genuinely novel, so candidates with backgrounds in statistical
mechanics, stochastic processes, analog/mixed-signal design, or novel
compute architectures are especially relevant.

Small team means high individual impact but also requires comfort with
early-stage risk and ambiguity. Research-oriented culture given the
fundamental nature of the technology.

## Recent Context

Taped out CN101, the world's first thermodynamic computing chip, in
August 2025. Roadmap includes CN201 (2026) for high-resolution
diffusion models and CN301 (late 2027) for video diffusion workloads.
Lawrence Berkeley National Lab published supporting research on
thermodynamic computing energy efficiency in early 2026.
