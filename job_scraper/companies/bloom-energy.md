# Bloom Energy

## Overview

Bloom Energy manufactures solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) systems that
convert natural gas or hydrogen into electricity through an electrochemical
process, without combustion. The company's Energy Server platform provides
on-site, always-on power generation for commercial, industrial, and data
center customers, with growing demand from AI infrastructure.

## Technical Focus

- Solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) design and manufacturing
- Natural gas and hydrogen fuel cell power generation
- On-site distributed power for data centers and C&I
- Electrochemical materials science and ceramics engineering
- Power electronics and grid interconnection

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: BE). Record FY2025 revenue of $2.02B (up 37% YoY). ~2,200
employees. Scaling to 2 GW annual production capacity by end of 2026
(~$100M investment). Multi-billion-dollar backlog driven by data center
and AI demand. Stock up ~1,000% in one year.

## Hiring

Bloom Energy hires across electrochemistry, materials science, mechanical
engineering, power electronics, manufacturing, and software. Core R&D
roles focus on fuel cell stack design, ceramic materials, and
electrochemical performance. Manufacturing roles span cell fabrication,
stack assembly, and quality engineering as production capacity doubles.

The AI data center boom has accelerated hiring, particularly in project
engineering, field deployment, and systems integration. Bloom values deep
technical expertise in fuel cell or adjacent electrochemical domains for
R&D roles, while platform and deployment roles benefit from experience
with distributed power systems and data center infrastructure.

## Recent Context

Bloom Energy reported record 2025 revenue of $2.02B, driven by surging
AI data center demand. The company signed a $5B partnership with Brookfield
to deploy fuel cells at AI data centers globally and partnered with SK
Eternix for the world's largest fuel cell installation in South Korea.
Bloom is on track to double production capacity to 2 GW by end of 2026.
