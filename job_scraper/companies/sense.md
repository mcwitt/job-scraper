# Sense

## Overview

Sense builds AI-powered home energy monitoring technology
that identifies individual device usage from electrical
signals. Starting as a direct-to-consumer monitor installed
in over 100K homes, Sense now embeds its energy
disaggregation technology into smart meters and electrical
panels through partnerships with utilities and hardware
OEMs. Founded in 2013 by Mike Phillips (CEO), Chris Micali,
and Ryan Houlette, Sense is headquartered in Cambridge, MA.

## Technical Focus

- AI/ML-based energy disaggregation (device-level detection)
- Smart meter embedded analytics (Landis+Gyr partnership)
- Smart electrical panel integration (Schneider Electric)
- Real-time home energy monitoring app
- Utility grid management and customer engagement software
- Edge and cloud signal processing

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised $195M total over 8 rounds including a
Series D (Jan 2026, with Analog Devices as strategic
investor). Earlier $105M round in 2022 for scaling smart
meter integrations. Approximately 106 employees. Deployed
in 100K+ homes with growing utility channel.

## Hiring

Sense hires ML engineers, embedded systems engineers,
firmware developers, and full-stack software engineers.
The core technology is signal processing and machine
learning for energy disaggregation, so experience in
time-series analysis, edge computing, or IoT is valuable.
The utility channel expansion also creates roles in
partnerships and business development.

## Recent Context

Sense closed a Series D in Jan 2026 with strategic
investment from Analog Devices, reinforcing its pivot
toward embedded utility-grade solutions. The company
continues expanding partnerships with smart meter makers
and electrical equipment manufacturers to bring real-time
energy data to the mass market.
