# Parallel Systems

## Overview

Builds autonomous battery-electric rail vehicles for freight
transport, aiming to shift short-haul cargo from trucks to
rail. Founded in 2020 by Matt Soule, John Howard, and Ben
Stabler — all former SpaceX engineers. Headquartered in
Los Angeles, California.

## Technical Focus

- Autonomous battery-electric rail vehicles (Gen 3)
- Self-sorting platoon formation without couplers
- Train control systems and autonomy software
- Positive Train Control (PTC) integration
- Intermodal container transport (up to 500 mi range)
- Compatibility with existing rail infrastructure

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B. Raised $38M Series B (2025) led by
Anthos Capital with Collaborative Fund, Congruent Ventures,
and Riot Ventures. Total funding over $100M. Approximately
44 employees. Backlog of 300+ vehicles with leading
railroads; targeting initial commercial operations in 2026.

## Hiring

Hardware-meets-software startup with strong SpaceX DNA.
Expect roles spanning robotics, embedded systems, autonomy,
mechanical engineering, and rail operations. Small team
means broad scope and high ownership per person. Technical
bar is high given safety-critical autonomous vehicle work
on live rail networks.

## Recent Context

Received FRA approval in 2025 for first commercial pilot.
Partnered with Genesee & Wyoming (G&W) to test on 160-mile
stretch of two Georgia short lines; Phase 1 completed 90
miles passing all safety milestones, Phase 2 under way.
Previously validated PTC compatibility with Union Pacific.
Scaling production of Gen 3 vehicle and accompanying
autonomy stack.
