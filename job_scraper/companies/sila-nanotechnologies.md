# Sila Nanotechnologies

## Overview

Sila Nanotechnologies develops advanced silicon anode materials for
lithium-ion batteries that replace traditional graphite, enabling
higher energy density and faster charging. The company's flagship
product, Titan Silicon, is designed for automotive-scale EV battery
production.

## Technical Focus

- Silicon anode material science and chemistry (Titan Silicon)
- Battery cell engineering and integration
- Advanced manufacturing at scale (Moses Lake, WA plant)
- Materials characterization and quality control

## Scale & Stage

Private, valued at ~$3.3B. ~395 employees. Raised $1.31B total across
13 rounds, including $375M Series G in 2024. Received $100M DOE award
for manufacturing scale-up. Major customer contracts with
Mercedes-Benz, Panasonic, and three additional undisclosed OEMs.

## Hiring

Sila hires across materials science, chemical engineering, process
engineering, manufacturing, and software. The company is transitioning
from R&D to production scale-up, so roles increasingly focus on
manufacturing operations, quality, and supply chain alongside
continued materials research.

The technical bar is high, particularly for materials and chemistry
roles where domain expertise is essential. Software and data roles
support manufacturing automation and process optimization. The Moses
Lake factory buildout has driven significant hiring in operations and
engineering.

## Recent Context

Sila completed its Moses Lake manufacturing plant in Q1 2025 and began
delivering Titan Silicon to automotive customers in Q4 2025. The
company is in the critical transition from pilot-scale to commercial
production, with contracts in place to supply next-generation EV
batteries for Mercedes-Benz and other OEMs.
