# Lightmatter

## Overview

Photonic computing company building optical interconnects and
accelerators for AI data centers. Uses silicon photonics to move
data between chips at higher bandwidth and lower energy than
electronic interconnects. Founded in 2017 by three MIT alumni
(Nicholas Harris, Darius Bunandar, Thomas Graham) who won the
MIT $100K Entrepreneurship Competition.

## Technical Focus

- Passage M1000: 3D photonic interposer with 114 Tbps bandwidth
  for connecting arrays of accelerators and switches
- Passage L200: co-packaged optics (CPO) platform with 32-64+
  Tbps for next-gen XPU and switch silicon
- Envise: general-purpose photonic AI accelerator combining
  electronic memory with optical matrix multiplication
- Idiom: software layer for running deep learning models on
  photonic hardware
- Manufacturing via GlobalFoundries partnership

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series D. Raised $400M Series D in October 2024 at
$4.4B valuation ($850M total). Led by T. Rowe Price, with
Fidelity and Google Ventures participating. ~315 employees as of
early 2026, up from ~228 in mid-2025. Actively hiring.

## Hiring

Hardware-heavy organization hiring chip designers, photonics
engineers, packaging engineers, and software developers. ~50 open
roles as of early 2026. Based in Mountain View, CA. Roles span
chip design, hardware engineering, software development, and
manufacturing partnerships.

Unique technical skillset required: intersection of photonics,
semiconductor packaging, and AI systems. Strong academic ties
given MIT origins.

## Recent Context

Announced Passage M1000 in March 2025 with 114 Tbps optical
bandwidth. Partnered with GlobalFoundries to manufacture photonic
interconnects. Positioned to address a critical bottleneck as AI
training clusters scale beyond 100,000 accelerators and electronic
interconnects hit bandwidth and power limits. Growing headcount
rapidly post-Series D.
