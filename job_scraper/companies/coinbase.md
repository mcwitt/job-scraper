# Coinbase

## Overview

Largest U.S.-based cryptocurrency exchange, operating in
100+ countries with 108M+ customers. Provides trading,
custody, staking, and institutional services for digital
assets. Founded in 2012 by Brian Armstrong and Fred Ehrsam.
Remote-first company (no formal HQ since 2020); originally
based in San Francisco.

## Technical Focus

- Base: Ethereum L2 blockchain built on the OP Stack,
  leading L2 by revenue and DeFi TVL
- Go microservices with gRPC, monorepo built with Bazel
- Infrastructure on Kubernetes, Docker, Terraform, EC2
- Onchain platform (Base App) for trading, wallets, DeFi
- Institutional-grade custody and staking infrastructure
- USDC stablecoin ecosystem (co-created via Circle)

## Scale & Stage

Public (NASDAQ: COIN). ~4,950 employees. 2025 revenue
$7.2B, up ~9% YoY. Market cap ~$55B. Holds ~$516B in
assets under custody including ~12% of all Bitcoin and
~11% of all staked Ether.

## Hiring

Remote-first with required in-person offsites multiple
times per year. Engineering roles span consumer products,
infrastructure, platform, and blockchain (Base). Values
intellectual curiosity, blameless truth-seeking, and
mission alignment. Hiring across compliance, engineering,
and customer support; added ~1,000 U.S. roles in 2025.

## Recent Context

Launched stock and ETF trading on the main app, expanding
into a multi-asset "Everything Exchange" (early 2026).
Acquiring The Clearing Company to scale prediction markets.
Base L2 stablecoin market cap hit $5.2B; bridged to Solana.
Bank of America upgraded COIN to Buy (January 2026).
Partnered with Standard Chartered for institutional crypto
services. Base App launched globally as an onchain super app.
