# Stripe

## Overview

Stripe builds financial infrastructure for the internet,
providing APIs and tools that enable businesses to accept
payments, manage subscriptions, send payouts, and handle
complex financial operations. Founded in 2010 by brothers
Patrick and John Collison, Stripe is dual-headquartered in
South San Francisco, CA and Dublin, Ireland.

## Technical Focus

- Payments API and processing infrastructure (500M+ API
  requests/day)
- Billing, subscriptions, and revenue recognition
- Connect platform for marketplace payments
- Fraud detection and risk management (Radar)
- Identity verification and compliance tooling
- Stablecoin payments (USDC on Ethereum, Solana, Polygon)
- Developer tools, SDKs, and Workbench dashboard

## Scale & Stage

Private, valued at $159B (Feb 2026 tender offer). Raised
$9.8B total over 24 rounds. Approximately 8,500 employees.
Processed $1.9T in total payment volume in 2025 (up 34%
YoY). Revenue suite on track for $1B annual run rate in
2026.

## Hiring

Stripe hires across engineering, infrastructure, product,
design, data science, and go-to-market. Engineering roles
span payments, platform, machine learning, and developer
experience. The bar is high — Stripe is known for rigorous
technical interviews and a culture that values clear
writing. Remote and hybrid options available across many
roles.

## Recent Context

Stripe's valuation soared 74% to $159B in a Feb 2026 tender
offer for employees and shareholders. The company added
stablecoin payment support and AI-powered developer tools
in 2025. Stripe also led PostHog's $70M Series D, signaling
interest in developer-focused investments.
