# EnCharge AI

## Overview

Fabless semiconductor company building analog
in-memory-computing AI chips for energy-efficient
edge inference. Incubated at Princeton University,
co-founded by Naveen Verma (Princeton EE professor)
and Kailash Gopalakrishnan (former IBM Fellow).
Founded in 2022, headquartered in Santa Clara, CA.

## Technical Focus

- Analog in-memory computing for AI inference
- Charge-based memory (capacitor-based, ~20x more
  energy-efficient than traditional GPUs)
- EN100 accelerator: 200+ TOPS in 8.25W (M.2) and
  ~1 POPS (PCIe with 4 NPUs)
- Edge and client computing (laptops, workstations)

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B. Raised $100M Series B in February
2025 led by Tiger Global, with Samsung VC, HH-CTBC
(Foxconn/CTBC), and RTX Ventures. Total funding
~$144M. Approximately 50-60 employees.

## Hiring

Hardware-focused team hiring across chip design,
analog/mixed-signal engineering, firmware, and AI
software. Rapid growth expected as EN100 moves to
commercialization. Values deep expertise in
semiconductor design and ML acceleration.

## Recent Context

Announced the EN100 accelerator in May 2025, the
company's first commercial product. Available in M.2
and PCIe form factors targeting on-device AI
inference. Series B was oversubscribed, signaling
strong investor confidence in analog AI compute.
