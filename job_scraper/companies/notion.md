# Notion

## Overview

Notion is a connected workspace that combines notes, docs,
wikis, project management, and databases into a single
platform. Founded in 2013 by Ivan Zhao and Simon Last,
Notion is headquartered in San Francisco, CA. The product
serves over 4 million customers ranging from individuals to
large enterprises.

## Technical Focus

- All-in-one workspace (docs, wikis, databases, projects)
- AI agents for autonomous multi-step workflows (Notion 3.0)
- Enterprise search across workspace and connected apps
- Real-time collaboration and permission systems
- MCP (Model Context Protocol) integrations
- Multi-model AI (GPT-5, Claude, o3) embedded in platform

## Scale & Stage

Private, valued at $11B (Dec 2025 tender offer). Raised
$418M total over 9 rounds; last primary round was a $275M
Series D in 2021. Estimated $500M+ ARR as of late 2025.
Approximately 1,200-1,500 employees.

## Hiring

Notion hires engineers, product managers, designers, and
go-to-market roles. Engineering spans frontend, backend,
infrastructure, AI/ML, and mobile. The company values
craft and product taste — candidates are expected to care
deeply about user experience. AI is a major hiring focus
following the Notion 3.0 launch with autonomous agents.

## Recent Context

Notion 3.0 launched in Sep 2025 with autonomous AI agents
capable of executing multi-step workflows across hundreds
of pages. Notion 3.2 (Jan 2026) brought mobile AI and new
model support. AI is now bundled into Business and
Enterprise plans at no extra cost, driving adoption.
