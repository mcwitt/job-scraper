# Heron Power

## Overview

Heron Power develops solid-state transformer technology to
modernize the electrical grid. Founded in 2024 by Drew
Baglino, former Tesla SVP of Powertrain and Energy (2006-
2024), who led development of Tesla's 4680 battery cell
and 50 GWh battery factory. Headquartered in Scotts Valley,
CA.

## Technical Focus

- Solid-state transformers (Heron Link product)
- Wide-bandgap semiconductor power electronics
- Modular megawatt-scale power converters
- Medium-voltage grid interconnection
- Software-integrated grid infrastructure
- High-volume power electronics manufacturing

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series B. Raised $38M Series A (May 2025) led by
Capricorn Investment Group, then $140M Series B (Feb 2026)
co-led by a16z American Dynamism and Breakthrough Energy
Ventures. Total funding ~$183M. Approximately 70 employees.
Planning a factory for 40 GW annual transformer production.

## Hiring

Heron Power hires power electronics engineers, software
architects, and manufacturing specialists. The founder's
deep Tesla pedigree (18 years) sets a high technical bar,
especially for power electronics and energy systems roles.
Early-stage culture focused on rapid product development
and manufacturing scale-up. JB Straubel (Tesla co-founder)
and former Tesla CFO Zach Kirkhorn are angel investors,
reflecting the Tesla alumni network around the company.

## Recent Context

Closed $140M Series B in Feb 2026 to build a US factory
for its Heron Link solid-state transformers. Customers have
expressed interest in over 40 GW of capacity, driven by
AI data center power demand and grid congestion. The Heron
Link replaces traditional copper-wound transformers with
semiconductor-based power conversion, addressing a critical
transformer shortage that constrains grid expansion.
