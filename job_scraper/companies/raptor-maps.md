# Raptor Maps

## Overview

Raptor Maps builds lifecycle management software for the
solar industry, helping asset owners and operators convert
drone inspection data, thermal imagery, and operational
metrics into actionable analytics. Founded in 2015 by
Nikhil Vadhavkar (CEO), Eddie Obropta (CTO), and Forrest
Meyen — all MIT graduates — the company is headquartered
in Somerville, MA. YC S16 alum.

## Technical Focus

- Solar asset lifecycle management platform
- Drone and aerial thermal/color imagery processing
- Anomaly detection and defect classification (ML/CV)
- Portfolio-level performance analytics
- Work order management and field operations
- Integration with SCADA and monitoring systems

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised $63M total including a $35M Series C
(Dec 2024, led by Maverix Private Equity) with participation
from Blue Bear Capital, Congruent Ventures, Buoyant
Ventures, and Y Combinator. Manages analytics across a
significant share of US utility-scale solar assets.

## Hiring

Raptor Maps hires software engineers, data scientists, ML
engineers, and solar industry specialists. The platform
combines computer vision, geospatial data, and operational
workflows, so candidates with experience in imagery
analysis, time-series data, or energy asset management are
a strong fit. The team is small and engineering-heavy.

## Recent Context

Raptor Maps closed a $35M Series C in Dec 2024 to drive
next-generation solar asset management. The company is
building toward an integrated operating system for the
solar industry as the US solar fleet continues rapid
expansion.
