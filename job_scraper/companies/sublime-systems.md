# Sublime Systems

## Overview

Sublime Systems reinvents cement manufacturing using a
proprietary electrochemical process that eliminates CO2
emissions from both fuel combustion and limestone
calcination. Founded in 2020 by Dr. Leah Ellis and
Prof. Yet-Ming Chiang (MIT), the company is headquartered
in Somerville, MA. Its "true-zero" approach produces
ASTM C1157-compliant cement as a drop-in replacement
for ordinary portland cement.

## Technical Focus

- Electrochemical extraction of calcium and silicates
- Ambient-temperature cement production (no kilns)
- Carbon-free raw material feedstocks
- Process electrification with clean electricity
- Concrete materials science and standards compliance

## Scale & Stage

Private. Raised over $200M total including a combined
$75M from strategic investors CRH and Holcim in 2024,
plus an $87M DOE award (later rescinded). Around 115-130
employees after a ~10% workforce reduction in late 2025.
Backed by Lowercarbon Capital, Engine Ventures,
Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Microsoft, and others.

## Hiring

Sublime hires across electrochemistry, materials science,
process engineering, and manufacturing scale-up. The
company values candidates with hardware and deep-tech
backgrounds who can move between R&D and
commercialization. Culture is mission-driven with a
climate-tech urgency. Software roles support process
controls and data infrastructure.

## Recent Context

Sublime paused construction of its planned 30,000 ton/yr
commercial plant in Holyoke, MA after the DOE rescinded
its $87M OCED grant in late 2025, triggering a 10%
workforce reduction. The company is pursuing alternative
scale-up plans. Microsoft has signaled intent to purchase
environmental attributes from Sublime's cement. CRH and
Holcim remain strategic investors and partners.
