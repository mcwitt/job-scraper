# Orbital

## Overview

AI-first hardware company designing data center infrastructure from
the atomic level up. Uses AI to simulate materials, test millions of
hardware configurations, and produce cooling and compute systems with
breakthrough real-world performance. Founded as Orbital Materials,
operating as Orbital (orbitalindustries.com).

## Technical Focus

- AI-driven materials discovery and molecular modeling (Orb platform)
- Data center thermal management: direct-to-chip two-phase liquid cooling
- Modular data center design (Nova Array) for high-density AI compute
- Hardware-AI feedback loop: deployed hardware generates field data to improve models
- ML research, mechanical engineering, and chemical engineering teams

## Scale & Stage

Private, seed stage. Based in London with presence in Canada and USA.
Raised $4.8M seed (July 2023) from Fly Ventures, Compound, and Flying
Fish Ventures. Small team — founded 2022 by James Gin, Jonathan
Godwin, and Daniel Miodovnik. Leadership from AWS, Microsoft, and
DeepMind backgrounds.

## Hiring

Early-stage team hiring across ML research, product, mechanical
engineering, and chemical engineering. Given the interdisciplinary
nature of the work, candidates likely need depth in either AI/ML or
physical sciences/engineering. Small team means high ownership and
broad scope per person.

Founding team brings experience scaling infrastructure at hyperscalers
(AWS, Microsoft, DeepMind), suggesting a preference for candidates
who combine research ability with practical engineering execution.

## Recent Context

Launching Nova Array modular data center (Q1 2026) and direct-to-chip
cooling system (Q4 2026) targeting PUE as low as 1.04. Extended Orb
platform to molecular systems (OrbMol). Partnered with AWS for data
center decarbonization and recognized by The Economist for AI-designed
materials.
