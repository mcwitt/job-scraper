# Fivetran

## Overview

Fivetran provides an automated data integration
platform that extracts data from hundreds of sources,
loads it into cloud warehouses, and supports ELT
(Extract, Load, Transform) workflows. Founded in
2012 by George Fraser and Taylor Brown, the company
is headquartered in Oakland, CA. Fivetran is
approaching $600M in combined ARR following its
merger agreement with dbt Labs.

## Technical Focus

- Automated connectors for 300+ data sources
- ELT pipeline management and monitoring
- Incremental data sync and CDC (change data capture)
- Reverse ETL (via Census acquisition, May 2025)
- Data transformation (via Tobiko Data acquisition)

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised ~$730M total across 6 rounds. Became
a unicorn in 2021. Approximately 1,700 employees as
of early 2026. Founded 2012.

## Hiring

Fivetran hires across engineering, product, sales,
and customer success. The engineering team builds
and maintains hundreds of data connectors, pipeline
infrastructure, and the cloud platform. Oakland HQ
with global offices.

## Recent Context

In October 2025, Fivetran and dbt Labs announced an
all-stock merger to form a combined data infrastructure
company, with George Fraser as CEO and Tristan Handy
as co-founder and president. Earlier in 2025, Fivetran
acquired Census (reverse ETL, May) and Tobiko Data
(open-source transformation) to expand its platform
capabilities for AI and analytics workloads.
