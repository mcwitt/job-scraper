# Netflix

## Overview

World's leading streaming entertainment service with 325+ million paid
memberships across 190+ countries. Content spans TV series, films,
documentaries, and mobile games. Expanding into live events, ad-supported
tiers, and gaming.

## Technical Focus

- Streaming infrastructure: adaptive bitrate, CDN (Open Connect), live pipelines
- Recommendation and personalization systems (unified multi-task AI models)
- Microservices architecture on AWS at massive scale
- Media ML and data engineering (video, audio, text processing)
- Content delivery to 100M+ simultaneous devices

## Scale & Stage

Public (NASDAQ: NFLX). ~14,000 employees. FY2025 revenue $45B (up 16%
YoY). Ad revenue exceeded $1.5B in 2025. Guiding for $50-52B revenue
in 2026 with ~$20B content spend. Operating margin targeting 31.5%.

## Hiring

Historically hired only senior engineers for 25 years — introduced
levels and new-grad hiring in 2023. Extremely high bar with under 2%
acceptance rate for SWE roles. Pays at the very top of market (~$266K
new grad, ~$520K senior). Expects strong ownership, technical depth,
and cultural alignment.

"People over process" culture with high autonomy and continuous
feedback instead of formal reviews. Engineers make decisions without
layers of approval. Freedom and responsibility model means high
performers thrive while underperformers are quickly managed out.

## Recent Context

Reached 325M subscribers in Q4 2025. Built out live streaming
infrastructure for global events. Merging recommendation pipelines
into a unified multi-task AI foundation model for cross-domain
personalization.
