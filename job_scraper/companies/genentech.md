# Genentech

## Overview

Pioneer of the biotechnology industry. Founded in 1976
by biochemist Herbert Boyer and venture capitalist
Robert Swanson. Headquartered in South San Francisco.
Became a wholly owned subsidiary of Roche in 2009.
Develops and commercializes medicines for oncology,
immunology, neuroscience, ophthalmology, and rare
diseases.

## Technical Focus

- Monoclonal antibodies and bispecific antibodies
- Immuno-oncology (checkpoint inhibitors, ADCs)
- Gene therapy and RNA interference (RNAi)
- AI/ML drug discovery (Prescient Design, NVIDIA
  collaboration)
- Computational biology (400+ person gRED Comp
  Sciences)
- Large-scale genomic and clinical data platforms
- Biologics manufacturing and process engineering

## Scale & Stage

Wholly owned subsidiary of Roche (ROG). Roche Group
revenue ~$65B (2025). Genentech employs ~25,000 people,
primarily in South San Francisco and North Carolina.
Operates one of the largest biotech R&D organizations
globally.

## Hiring

Roles span research, computational sciences, clinical
development, manufacturing, and commercial. Heavy demand
for ML engineers, computational biologists, and data
scientists within gRED. Wet-lab and translational science
roles require deep domain expertise. Culture emphasizes
scientific rigor, collaboration, and long-term patient
impact.

## Recent Context

FDA approved Tecentriq + lurbinectedin for first-line
SCLC maintenance (Oct 2025). Filed NDA for giredestrant
in ESR1-mutated breast cancer (Feb 2026). Signed $1.7B
RNAi licensing deal with SanegeneBio. Expanding Holly
Springs, NC manufacturing site. Announced $50B US
investment in manufacturing, infrastructure, and R&D.
