# Liquid AI

## Overview

AI research company building foundation models based on liquid neural
networks, a novel architecture invented by the founding team at MIT CSAIL.
Liquid networks use continuous-time dynamics rather than fixed-step updates,
enabling compact, interpretable models that remain adaptable after training.
Spun out of MIT in 2023.

## Technical Focus

- Liquid neural network architecture research and development
- Liquid foundation models (LFMs) for language, vision, and multimodal tasks
- Efficient inference for edge and real-time applications
- Model architectures that are compact, causal, and interpretable

## Scale & Stage

Private, Series A. Raised $250M Series A in December 2024 led by AMD ($297M
total), valued at $2B. ~88 employees. Founded by MIT CSAIL researchers
including Daniela Rus (CSAIL director), Ramin Hasani, Mathias Lechner, and
Alexander Amini.

## Hiring

Research-heavy organization rooted in novel neural network architectures.
Hiring researchers and engineers with experience in sequence modeling,
efficient architectures, systems optimization, and foundation model
training.

Small team with strong academic pedigree (MIT CSAIL origins). High research
bar with emphasis on architectural innovation over scaling existing
approaches. Expect deep technical interviews focused on ML fundamentals
and novel model design.

## Recent Context

Released liquid foundation models with 1B, 3B, and 40B parameter variants.
The 40B model outperformed Meta's Llama 3.1 70B on MMLU-Pro benchmarks
despite being nearly half the size, demonstrating the efficiency advantages
of the liquid architecture. Growing revenue ($13M ARR as of late 2025) with
enterprise customers adopting LFMs for edge and latency-sensitive workloads.
