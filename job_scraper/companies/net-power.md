# NET Power

## Overview

NET Power develops zero-emission natural gas power plants
using the proprietary Allam-Fetvedt Cycle, a supercritical
CO2 power cycle that inherently captures over 97% of CO2
emissions. Founded in 2010, headquartered in Durham, NC.
CEO Danny Rice (co-founder of Rice Energy) led NET Power
through its 2023 SPAC merger (NYSE: NPWR).

## Technical Focus

- Supercritical CO2 (sCO2) power cycle design
- Oxy-combustion turbine technology
- Carbon capture, utilization, and sequestration
- High-efficiency natural gas power generation (~55% net)
- Partnership with Baker Hughes on sCO2 turboexpanders
- Utility-scale clean baseload power deployment

## Scale & Stage

Public (NYSE: NPWR) since June 2023 via SPAC merger at
$1.5B valuation. Approximately $379M cash on hand as of
Q4 2025. Pre-revenue; first utility-scale plant (Project
Permian, 80 MW) under development near Odessa, TX, with
targeted commercial operations in early 2029. Estimated
100-200 employees.

## Hiring

NET Power hires across power plant engineering, project
development, turbomachinery, process engineering, and
corporate functions. Given the deep-tech nature of the
Allam Cycle, roles emphasize thermal systems, mechanical
engineering, and power systems expertise. The company is
in a critical scale-up phase, transitioning from R&D
demonstration to first commercial deployment.

## Recent Context

In Q4 2025, NET Power increased Project Permian's net
output target from 60 MW to 80 MW, with turbine delivery
expected early 2028 and final investment decision in H2
2026. The company is progressing a strategic partnership
with Entropy Inc., targeting definitive agreements in Q2
2026. A UK-based Allam Cycle plant is also taking shape,
broadening international deployment interest.
