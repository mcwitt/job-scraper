# Toyota Research Institute

## Overview

R&D subsidiary of Toyota Motor Corporation focused on AI, robotics,
autonomous driving, and materials science. Founded in 2016 with a $1B
investment, TRI operates as an independent research lab that develops
technologies for eventual integration into Toyota products and broader
societal benefit.

## Technical Focus

- Large Behavior Models (LBMs) for robotic manipulation
- Autonomous driving and active safety (Human Interactive Driving)
- Materials science and energy (battery materials, catalysts)
- Foundation models for robotics (vision-language-action models)
- Human-robot interaction and dexterous manipulation

## Scale & Stage

Subsidiary of Toyota Motor Corporation (public, TYO: 7203). ~430
employees across Los Altos (HQ), Cambridge MA, and international
offices. Funded by Toyota with multi-billion-dollar backing. Not a
startup -- operates with the resources of a major automaker but the
culture of a research lab.

## Hiring

High technical bar with emphasis on ML research, robotics, and
scientific computing. Roles span research scientists, ML engineers,
and robotics engineers. Publishes at top venues and values candidates
with strong research backgrounds. Average hiring process takes ~29
days.

Culture blends academic research freedom with industry-scale resources.
Active internship program (Summer 2026 positions currently open).
Collaboration with academic partners (Stanford, Boston Dynamics)
provides exposure to cutting-edge research problems.

## Recent Context

Partnership with Boston Dynamics demonstrated Large Behavior Models
powering the Atlas humanoid robot for complex manipulation tasks in
August 2025. Toyota's joint venture with Pony.ai began mass production
of robotaxi vehicles in February 2026. TRI's "Driving Sensei" concept
uses AI to teach driving skills through AI-driven instruction and
real-time driver support.
