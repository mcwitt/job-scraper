# Form Energy

## Overview

Form Energy develops iron-air batteries for multi-day grid-scale energy
storage, capable of discharging for up to 100 hours. Headquartered in
Somerville, MA, the company uses a reversible rusting process with
low-cost iron as the primary storage medium, targeting the long-duration
storage gap that lithium-ion cannot economically fill.

## Technical Focus

- Iron-air battery electrochemistry
- Multi-day energy storage system design (100-hour discharge)
- High-volume battery manufacturing
- Grid integration and utility-scale deployment
- Materials science with abundant, low-cost inputs (iron, water, air)

## Scale & Stage

Private, raised over $1.2B total including a $405M Series F. Over 900
employees. Manufacturing at Form Factory 1 in Weirton, WV, ramping to
500 MW/year capacity by 2028. Major utility customers include Xcel Energy,
Georgia Power, Great River Energy, and Dominion Energy. Founded 2017.

## Hiring

Form Energy hires across battery R&D, manufacturing engineering, project
development, and software. The company values candidates who can contribute
to scaling novel hardware from pilot to mass production. Electrochemistry,
materials science, and manufacturing process experience are core, but they
also hire software engineers for factory automation and grid integration.

With 900+ employees and a large factory ramping in West Virginia, Form is
past the early-startup phase and operates more like a growth-stage
industrial company. Roles span lab research, factory floor operations,
supply chain, project engineering, and corporate functions.

## Recent Context

In February 2026, Google and Xcel Energy announced the world's largest
battery project: a 300 MW / 30 GWh Form Energy iron-air system in Pine
Island, Minnesota, to power Google data centers with 100-hour discharge
capability. Form's first commercial projects with Great River Energy and
Dominion Energy are coming online in 2025-2026, and the Weirton factory
is actively ramping production.
