# LinkedIn

## Overview

World's largest professional networking platform with 1B+
members globally. Founded by Reid Hoffman in 2002, launched
publicly in 2003. Acquired by Microsoft in 2016 for $26.2B.
Headquartered in Sunnyvale, CA with major offices in San
Francisco, New York, Dublin, Bengaluru, and Seattle.

## Technical Focus

- Economic graph and large-scale graph systems
- Feed ranking and recommendation algorithms
- Search infrastructure and retrieval systems
- AI/ML platform (generative AI, NLP, computer vision)
- Real-time data infrastructure (Kafka, Samza, Brooklin)
- Distributed systems (Espresso, Ambry, Venice)
- Frontend at scale (Ember, server-driven UI)
- AI agents (Hiring Assistant, conversational search)
- Data analytics and experimentation platforms

## Scale & Stage

Subsidiary of Microsoft (public, MSFT). ~21,000 employees
as of 2025, with ~18% in the SF Bay Area. Revenue exceeds
$15B annually, primarily from Talent Solutions, Marketing
Solutions, and Premium Subscriptions. Operates at massive
scale serving 1B+ members across 200+ countries.

## Hiring

Large engineering org across infrastructure, AI/ML, search,
feed, security, and product. Interview bar is high — expect
system design depth and strong coding fundamentals. Culture
emphasizes trust, collaboration, and transformation. Comp
is competitive with Big Tech (Microsoft RSUs). Hybrid work
model with some remote flexibility.

## Recent Context

Heavily investing in generative AI across the platform.
Launched AI-powered Hiring Assistant (first large-scale AI
agent for recruiters) with global expansion in 2026. Added
AI conversational job search, collaborative articles, and
AI-assisted candidate discovery. Underwent layoffs in 2025
as part of broader Microsoft restructuring (~270 Bay Area
roles cut). Continues building out its GenAI platform
strategy with modular agent architectures.
